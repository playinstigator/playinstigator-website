Sample deck structure

A 5-slide scaffold for your ~8 min share. Use it exactly as-is, borrow from it, or ignore it entirely and do your own thing.

Format is open. Slides, a sketchbook, screenshots, WIP photos, voice notes, a Notion doc, printed pages on the table. Whatever best shows how you actually work. The slides below are a scaffold, not a script. The real value is the discussion after, so keep the share loose enough to invite reactions.
1 Who you are ~1 min
Name + medium
Your name. One word for your medium.
e.g. "Suren / tabletop games" or "Ann / music & composition"
What you've made
1 or 2 things you've built or released. Just the names.
e.g. "Burnout (board game) · 1,200 Kickstarter backers"
What drew you here
One sentence: what is it about this medium that keeps pulling you back? Not why you're good at it. Why you can't seem to leave it alone.
e.g. "Games are the only medium where the audience makes the story happen."
2 A project you want to walk us through ~2 min
Show it
A screenshot, photo, sketch, audio clip, video clip. Whatever it looks or sounds like. Not a logo. Show the work itself.
What it is
One or two lines on what the project is and what it does or asks of an audience.
The thinking behind it
Why this? What were you trying to do or say? What drew you to this particular form?
3 What makes it distinctly yours ~2 min
A constraint that shaped it
Budget, format, tool, platform, time: a real limit that pushed the work somewhere it wouldn't have gone otherwise. What did it force you to discover?
e.g. "No dialogue budget, so we made silence part of the experience." / "Printed to a single A5, which meant ruthless cutting."
The smallest unit of meaning in your craft
A brushstroke, a beat, a panel, a mechanic, a chord, a word: the atom your medium is built from. Show an example of it working.
A move you're proud of
Something specific in this project you'd point at and say "that one works." Show it, don't just describe it.
4 How you actually build ~2 min
Where a project starts
What is the first thing that exists when you begin? A feeling, an image, a rule, a character, a sound, a constraint. What comes first and why?
What you figure out last
What's the last thing to get decided? What can't you know at the start?
The categories you always think about
Every medium has its own building blocks. What are yours? What are the areas or elements that any project in your craft has to answer, whether you tackle them consciously or not?
Examples by medium:
Games: feel / mechanics / loop / narrative / player psychology
Music: mood / key / instrumentation / structure / texture / reference
Illustration: palette / medium / composition / line / concept / audience
Space / play design: flow / safety / discovery / material / age range / intent
Writing / fiction: voice / structure / stakes / world rules / what's unsaid
5 The cross-medium question ~1 min · this one fuels the discussion
What your medium can do that no other can
Not "what it's good at". What is only possible here? What experience or feeling can your medium create that no other medium can replicate?
e.g. "Music can bypass the thinking brain and hit the body directly." / "Games are the only form where the audience can fail and have to try again."
If you were building one world across multiple crafts from day 1
What would your medium need from the other crafts, upfront, before you start? What would you want to establish together, and what would you want to discover as you build? Where would you lead, and where would you follow?
e.g. "I'd need the world's emotional tone locked first, everything else I can derive. But I'd want the mechanics to stay open so the music can react to gameplay states."
