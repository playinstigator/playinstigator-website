Sharing Session · Invite

How do you build a world?

A small, casual session for people who make things across different mediums. No pitch. No project. Just process.

Hey — I'm putting together a small, casual session for people who make things across different mediums, and I'd love to have you.

The idea is simple: a handful of creators — writers, musicians, illustrators, game folks, whoever — in one room (or call), each sharing how we actually work. Not finished portfolios. The messy middle: how you world-build, how you get from a blank page to an idea, how you prototype, how you know when something's done, where you get stuck.

No pitch. No project. Nothing to sign up for. I'm not recruiting you for anything — I just think there's something genuinely useful and fun in watching how someone from a totally different medium (or even your own) thinks through the same creative problems you do. We rarely get to see each other's process.

I'm an indie game dev building a world I care about, and I'm curious whether a group of people from different crafts can spark off each other. This first session is me testing exactly that — low stakes, good company, mutual curiosity.

If that sounds like your kind of evening, come.

What it is

  • A casual share-your-process hangout
  • Cross-medium creators comparing how we work
  • Curiosity-driven, low-stakes, fun
  • A way to get a feel for each other

What it isn't

  • A pitch or recruitment
  • A commitment to any project
  • Paid, or about money at all
  • A deliverable or homework
