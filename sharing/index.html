How do you build a world?

A small, casual session for creators across different mediums to share how each of us thinks about world-building — and whether co-world-building together is something we'd want to try.

Worlds are almost always built by one craft and adapted into others. The writer makes the world, the game and music and comics arrive later — pre-shaped, adapting decisions already made. By the time a creator from another medium gets there, the world is closed.

Across mediums at the same time, with creators of each craft in the room from the start — almost nobody builds worlds that way. Even the people who'd want to try it have nowhere to find each other, and no low-stakes way to find out if they'd actually click.

What would happen if we did try? If a handful of us from different mediums got in a room and showed each other how we actually think about world-building. Not pitching anything — just seeing how each of us works. And then asking the question the usual model never lets us ask: could a group like this actually try co-building a world together?

That's what this is.

I'm putting together a small evening — four to six creators from different mediums, casual, no pitch. We share how we each think about world-building, then sit with the bigger question.

I'm an indie game dev. I think the worlds I love most could only have been built with people from other crafts in the room from the start — but I've never actually seen it happen. I want to find out what it looks like when we try. Low stakes, good company, mutual curiosity.

If that sounds like your kind of evening, come.

Rough shape of the evening

  1. Each creator, 5–10 min — informally walk us through how you approach world-building in your medium. Slides optional. Show one or two real examples from your process if useful.
  2. Cross-medium discussion — what's surprising, what overlaps, what's totally medium-specific.
  3. The open question — would any of us want to try co-world-building together? What would make it worth doing? What would kill it?

What it is

  • Each creator shares how they world-build, briefly
  • An honest discussion about co-world-building
  • Cross-medium curiosity, low stakes
  • A way to feel out creative chemistry

What it isn't

  • A pitch or recruitment
  • A commitment to any project
  • Paid, or about money at all
  • A pre-made world you're being asked to join
