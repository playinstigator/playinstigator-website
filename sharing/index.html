How do you build a world?

In person · *SCAPE Level 5, Singapore 5 July 2026· part of IdeasFest

I am tired of bad transmedia adaptations. A Disney movie becomes a huge box office success, so now you need to turn it into a live-action remake, storybooks, a Broadway show and a new theme park area. Was the original work created with those in mind? Probably not.

The magic of the original work often gets lost in translation, and the creators in other mediums are limited by "the movie did it this way".

But what if we flipped it? What if we brought together creators of animation, games, music, comics, toys — and had them co-create a world that's shaped by exactly that combination of mediums from the start? Not adapting one world into others. Building one together, from the inside, each craft shaping it with their unique strengths and limitations.

That's what I wanna explore.

I'd love for you to come and share a bit about your creation process. Bring a couple of slides — or whatever best communicates how you actually design and create. Where do you start? What do you consider when dreaming up a new idea? How do you expand upon that idea? What are the considerations? What makes an idea the most exciting for you?

Not a pitch. No need to be polished. Just a show-and-tell from each medium, in conversation with one another.

It's a couple of hours on 5 July 2026 at *SCAPE Level 5, as part of IdeasFest, so other people will drift in and out around us. The core is a handful of us doing short shares and comparing notes. Exact structure and timing within the day are still to be confirmed — I'll share details once they're locked in.

I'm an indie game dev. I think the worlds I love most could only have been built with people from other crafts in the room from the start, and I'm curious what we'd notice if we got together for a bit and showed each other how we work. No pressure, no commitment — just creators learning from each other.

If that sounds like your kind of thing, come by.
