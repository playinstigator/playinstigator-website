How do you build a world?

Most transmedia worlds get built the same way. A Disney movie becomes a huge success — so now you need a live-action remake, storybooks, a Broadway show, a new theme park area. Was the original work created with those in mind? Probably not. The world was made for one medium, and everything else had to fit itself to it.

One craft owns the world. Everything else adapts. The soul gets lost in translation, and the creators in other mediums only show up after the decisions are already made.

But what if we flipped it? What if we brought together creators of animation, games, music, comics, toys — and had them co-create a world that's shaped by exactly that combination of mediums from the start? Not adapting one world into others. Building one together, from the inside, each craft shaping it from where it sits.

That's what this is.

I'd love for you to come and share a bit about your creation process. Bring a couple of slides about how you actually work — your tools, your approach, where things start. Nothing polished. Show-and-tell from each medium, in conversation with each other.

It's a couple of hours as part of a larger event, so other people will drift in and out around us. The core is a handful of us doing short shares and comparing notes.
