Sharing Session · Co-World-Building

How do you build a world?

A small, casual session for creators across different mediums to share how each of us thinks about world-building — and whether co-world-building together is something we'd want to try.

Hey — I'm putting together a small, casual session for people who make things across different mediums, and I'd love to have you.

Here's the angle: each of us comes in and shares how we think about world-building in our craft. Not finished worlds. The way you actually approach it: where does a world begin for you? How does it cohere? How do you know when it has weight? How does your medium shape the kinds of worlds you can build, and the kinds you can't?

A writer and a musician and an illustrator and a game designer don't world-build the same way. They probably shouldn't. But seeing each other's approaches up close, even briefly — that's interesting in itself.

And then the bigger question: if a handful of us were to actually try building a world together, across our mediums — what would that even look like? What would it ask of us? What would we want from it? Not a pitch. Not a commitment. Just an honest conversation about whether co-world-building is something any of us is curious about.

I'm an indie game dev building a world I care about, and I want to see what happens when people from different crafts compare notes on this specific thing. Low stakes, good company, mutual curiosity.

If that sounds like your kind of evening, come.

Rough shape of the evening

  1. Each creator, 5–10 min — informally walk us through how you approach world-building in your medium. Slides optional. Show one or two real examples from your process if useful.
  2. Cross-medium discussion — what's surprising, what overlaps, what's totally medium-specific.
  3. The open question — would any of us want to try co-world-building together? What would make it worth doing? What would kill it?

What it is

  • Each creator shares how they world-build, briefly
  • An honest discussion about co-world-building
  • Cross-medium curiosity, low stakes
  • A way to feel out creative chemistry

What it isn't

  • A pitch or recruitment
  • A commitment to any project
  • Paid, or about money at all
  • A pre-made world you're being asked to join
