How to prep your share

A quick guide to the 5 July session: what it is, how the 2 hours flow, and how to put together your bit. Nothing here is mandatory, it's just so you know what you're walking into.

📍 MAGES Institute classroom, *SCAPE Level 5 📅 5 July 2026, 12 to 2pm

What this is

A small, closed group of creators across different mediums (games, tabletop, music, illustration, space design, more) walking each other through how we actually build, then taking one idea and running it across all our crafts together.

The problem we're testing: most collaborative creative projects end up with one medium owning the world (the game, the book, the IP) and everyone else adapting after the fact. By the time music, art, or gameplay gets added, the decisions are already locked. This room asks: what happens when the crafts are in together from the start? No audience, just the creators, so we can talk openly and compare honestly.

How the 2 hours flow

  1. Quick intros: your medium, and one thing you wanna learn from another craft.
  2. Short shares, ~8 min each: host goes first so everyone knows what the format feels like, then you. Walk us through how you actually build.
  3. Break
  4. Deep dive into each other's crafts: ask the questions you actually have, dig into what makes each medium tick, figure out where the processes rhyme and where they clash.
  5. "What if we built one world together?": we take one idea and run it across all our mediums, live.
  6. Wrap: what surprised you, and what you're taking away.

The short share is just the on-ramp. Most of the 2 hours is us actually digging in together, that's the fun part.

Your share

It's the opposite of a polished talk. About 5 to 8 minutes, a few slides, a sketchbook, screenshots, voice notes, whatever best shows how you really work. The share is just the on-ramp. Most of the 2 hours is us actually digging in together.

For a slide-by-slide scaffold you can use directly: playinstigator.com/sharing/deck/

Then come ready to react to everyone else's share. Don't script answers to everything. The cross-talk is where it gets good.

A few ground rules

  • It's closed and candid. No audience, no performing to a crowd. Speak freely.
  • Recording. I'll record it just to capture everyone's thoughts and write up a summary for the group. Anything public (like a YouTube recap) is opt-in, and I'll check with the room on the day before anything goes up.
  • No pressure, nothing polished. The share is small, the discussion is the value.
  • Not a pitch. We're here to compare how we make things, not to sell.

Want something to chew on beforehand? There's a menu of prompts at playinstigator.com/sharing/questions/. Totally optional fuel.

Bring how you actually make things. Bring a question. Bring nothing prepared at all. All fine, see you on the 5th.
