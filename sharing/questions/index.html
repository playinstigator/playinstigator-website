Sharing Session · Things to think about

A menu of prompts

If you want something to chew on before the 5 July session — here's a menu. Totally optional fuel.

How to use this: pick whichever resonate. Ignore the rest. You don't need to answer any of them — they're here to spark thinking, not script your share. Sequenced to start deep in your own process and slowly fan out toward cross-medium collaboration. Feel free to bring something not on the list at all.

Your actual process

  • Walk us through one project from blank page to "this is done" — what did the shape of it look like?
  • Walk us through one specific decision: what was on the table, what did you pick, why?
  • What's the part of your process that looks nothing like the polished output?
  • What tools do you reach for in the messy early stage vs. the locked-in late stage?
  • Who else is in the room (literally or figuratively) when you make decisions?
  • What's a habit / ritual / superstition in your process you'd defend even if it's "inefficient"?

Where ideas and worlds begin

  • What's the first thing that exists when you start a new world — a mood, a character, a rule, a place, a sound?
  • Where do your ideas usually come from — observation, accident, deliberate prompting, somewhere else entirely?
  • Do you start from a feeling and reverse-engineer the world, or start from a system and let the feeling emerge?
  • How much of the world is decided before you start making, vs. discovered while making?
  • Is there a moment you can point to when an idea stopped being "an idea" and became "a thing I'm actually making"?

The elements of your craft

  • What's the smallest unit of meaning in your craft — a frame, a beat, a panel, a verb, a silhouette?
  • What are the building blocks you reach for again and again?
  • What do you spend the most time on that nobody else sees?
  • What's the part of the craft that took you the longest to learn — and that you'd warn a beginner about?
  • What's a technique or move in your craft you're still trying to master?

What you struggle with & iterate on

  • What's the problem you keep solving in every project?
  • Show us a version-1 vs. version-final of something — what changed, and why?
  • What's the most expensive thing to change late in your process?
  • What kills momentum in your work more than anything else?
  • A story or feeling you've tried to express and couldn't — what did the failure teach you?
  • A constraint (budget, format, tool, platform) that shaped the work — for better or worse?

The audience in your process

  • When does the audience first enter your process — early sketches, late tests, never?
  • What's a piece of feedback that genuinely changed how you work?
  • What feedback do you ignore, and why?
  • How do you tell the difference between "the audience doesn't get it" and "the work isn't ready"?
  • What kind of attention does your medium ask for — focused, ambient, repeat, social?
  • How much does your audience need to bring to meet the work halfway?

Reflections & where you want to grow

  • What's a project you'd remake if you started today — and what would you do differently?
  • What did you used to believe about your medium that you don't anymore?
  • What's a piece of your own work you're still proud of years later? One that didn't work, that you learned from?
  • What's the most "you" thing you've ever made?
  • What would your work look like in five years if it kept evolving the way it has been?

What your medium does — and doesn't

  • What can your medium do that no other medium can?
  • What does your medium let the audience feel that's hard to get elsewhere?
  • What's a story or feeling your medium forces you to leave out, no matter how good you get?
  • Where do you hit the wall of "this would work better as a ___"?

Translation across mediums

  • When you consume another medium, what makes you think "that should have been a ___"?
  • A piece of work that was adapted across mediums and got better — what did the adaptation understand?
  • A piece of work that was adapted and lost its soul — what did it lose, specifically?
  • What part of your process do you think translates across mediums — and what doesn't?
  • If someone handed you the brief "adapt your work into [other medium]" — what's the first thing you'd protect, and the first thing you'd sacrifice?

Collaboration & what-ifs

  • Have you ever made something with someone from a different craft? What did you learn that you couldn't have learned alone?
  • What does "good collaboration" look like for you — and what does the breakdown look like?
  • What would another medium bring to your work that you can't bring yourself?
  • What kind of cross-medium collab would actually excite you — not as a hypothetical, but as something you'd want to make next year?
  • If you were dropped into a room with the other folks here and told "make one world together," what would you want decided in the first hour?

Bring what resonates. Bring something not on the list.
Bring nothing prepared at all. All fine.
