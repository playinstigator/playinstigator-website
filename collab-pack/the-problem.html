02 · 我们为什么要做这件事

The Problem. 问题所在。

Transmedia adaptations always feel like adaptations. Because they are. And the reason is structural — not a skill problem, not a budget problem. The people making the secondary work were never in the room when the real decisions happened.

跨媒介改编永远有一种"改编感"。因为它们就是改编。原因是结构性的——不是技术问题，不是预算问题。制作二次创作的人，从来没有在真正做决策的房间里。

The Default Model默认的模式

Almost every transmedia IP follows the same pattern. A game ships. It does well. The studio commissions a manga adaptation. A composer is brought in to score the anime. A comic artist illustrates a prequel story. Each of these creators is skilled. Each of them produces work that is technically good. And all of it feels downstream — because it is.

The composer scored what was in front of them. The comic artist illustrated a world they didn't build. The manga writer worked from a lore document written by someone else. Their work is constrained by creative decisions made before they arrived, by a team that wasn't thinking about their medium when those decisions were made.

The result is adaptation. It will always feel like adaptation, because structurally, that's what it is.

几乎每一个跨媒介 IP 都遵循同样的模式。游戏上线，成绩不错，工作室委托了一个漫画改编。作曲家被请来为动画配乐。漫画家画了一个前传故事。这些创作者每一个都很有才华。他们每一个人产出的作品在技术上都是好的。但所有东西都有一种"下游感"——因为它们就是下游。

作曲家为摆在他们面前的东西配乐。漫画家描绘了一个不是他们建造的世界。漫画编剧从别人写的世界设定文档里工作。他们的作品受制于在他们到来之前就已经做出的创作决定，由一个当时并没有在考虑他们的媒介的团队做出的决定。

结果是改编。它永远会像改编，因为在结构上，它就是改编。

Even the Gold Standard Has This Problem即便是黄金标准也有这个问题

The best transmedia consultancies in the world — firms like Starlight Runner Entertainment, who produced transmedia strategies for Halo, Transformers, Spider-Man, Pirates of the Caribbean, and Avatar — operate from a mythology document. A transmedia bible. The Halo bible reportedly ran to four binders, hundreds of pages each, encoding every canonical fact so that downstream writers, game designers, and filmmakers stay consistent.

This is excellent at what it does. It is the gold standard of transmedia coherence in the industry today.

But Starlight Runner is called in after the IP already exists in one medium. Their job is to record the world that already exists and give every other medium a consistent reference to adapt from. The downstream creators are skilled implementers working from a reference document. They are not co-authors of the world itself.

The gold standard's ceiling is the starting point of our model.

世界上最好的跨媒介顾问公司——比如 Starlight Runner Entertainment，他们为《光环》《变形金刚》《蜘蛛侠》《加勒比海盗》以及阿凡达制作了跨媒介策略——都基于一份"神话文档"运作。一本跨媒介圣经。据报道，《光环》的圣经厚达四本活页夹，每本数百页，把所有正典事实都编码在内，让下游的作家、游戏设计师和电影人保持一致。

这在它能做到的范围内是非常出色的。这是当今行业里跨媒介一致性的黄金标准。

但 Starlight Runner 是在 IP 已经在某个媒介里存在之后才被请进来的。他们的工作是记录已经存在的世界，并给每一种其他媒介一个可以参照改编的一致参考文本。下游创作者是熟练的执行者，在一份参考文档的基础上工作。他们不是这个世界本身的共同作者。

黄金标准的天花板，正是我们模型的起点。

The Major Studios Operate the Same Way大型工作室的运作方式也是如此

Disney's pattern is the clearest example. Frozen succeeds as a film, and only then does the IP expand into theme parks, merchandise, stage shows, and sequels. The pilot verifies the IP can work. It is a financial de-risking step, not a co-creation step. The world was authored by the film team. Every subsequent medium adapts to what the film decided.

The major studios know transmedia works. They do it constantly. But they always adapt, because they're organized by medium — separate film divisions, game studios, publishing imprints, each with its own hierarchy and incentives. A game studio is a game studio. Asking them to be a co-equal node in a multi-medium creative collective means surrendering the identity that makes them legible to their own ecosystem. Incumbents default to what worked before.

迪士尼的模式是最清晰的例子。《冰雪奇缘》作为电影成功了，之后这个 IP 才扩展进主题乐园、周边、舞台剧和续集。这个"试点"验证了这个 IP 是可行的。这是一个财务上的降低风险步骤，不是一个共同创作步骤。这个世界是由电影团队创作的。每一个后续媒介都在适应电影所做的决定。

大型工作室知道跨媒介是有效的。他们一直在这么做。但他们永远只是改编，因为他们是按照媒介来组织的——独立的电影部门、游戏工作室、出版品牌，每一个都有自己的层级和激励机制。一家游戏工作室就是一家游戏工作室。让他们成为一个跨媒介创作共同体中的对等节点，意味着放弃让他们在自己生态系统中可被辨认的那个身份。行业老手默认回到之前有效的东西。

What This Costs the Creators这对创作者造成的损失

Here's the part that rarely gets named directly.

If you're a composer brought in to score an IP that already exists, you have creative freedom within the brief — but no influence over what the brief is. The world's emotional logic, its themes, its core tensions — all of that was decided before you joined. Your job is to serve the work that's already there, not to shape what it becomes. The question "what should this world actually feel like?" was answered without you.

This is how most transmedia work happens for most of the people making it. Not because studios are malicious — it's just how the structure works. One medium originates. Every other medium adapts. The originators author the IP. The others execute it.

The creative ceiling for a downstream collaborator is: doing the best possible job within constraints you had no part in setting.

这里有一件很少被直接点出来的事情。

如果你是一位被请来为一个已经存在的 IP 配乐的作曲家，你在这份简报范围内有创作自由——但你对简报是什么没有影响力。这个世界的情感逻辑、它的主题、它的核心张力——所有这些都在你加入之前就已经被决定了。你的工作是服务那个已经存在的作品，而不是塑造它将成为什么。"这个世界实际上应该是什么感觉？"这个问题，是在没有你的情况下被回答的。

这就是大多数跨媒介工作对大多数参与其中的人来说是如何发生的。不是因为工作室是恶意的——只是结构就是这样运作的。一种媒介发起创作。其他所有媒介都在改编。发起者创作了 IP。其他人执行它。

一个下游合作者的创作天花板是：在你没有参与设定的约束条件下，尽可能地把工作做好。

The Idea That Never Happened那个从未发生的想法

Here's a loss nobody talks about, because it's invisible by definition.

The original IP was built by one team, in one medium, thinking about that medium. The world was designed without your medium in mind — because you weren't there yet. Then you arrive. You start working. And somewhere in that process, you see it: a connection, an angle, an approach that would make your work extraordinary. It would require one small change to what the original established — a lore detail, a story beat, something that could easily have gone either way if someone had been asked. Nobody was asked. The original shipped. It's canon. The community knows it. The window is closed.

The idea dies. Not because it was weak. Because it was good enough to matter, and it arrived too late to be possible. You find a workaround. The final work is fine — technically solid, professionally done. But somewhere between what you made and what you nearly made is a gap that the structure created and nobody noticed.

This happens constantly. Nobody talks about it because finished work doesn't carry the ghost of what it almost was.

有一种损失没有人谈论，因为它从定义上就是不可见的。

原始 IP 由一个团队在一种媒介里建立，他们只考虑那种媒介。这个世界在设计时没有考虑到你的媒介——因为你当时还不在。然后你到来了。你开始工作。在这个过程中的某个时刻，你看到了它：一个联系，一个角度，一种方式，可以让你的作品变得非凡。它只需要对原作已经建立的东西做一个小小的改动——一个世界观细节，一个故事节拍，某个如果有人问过就很容易可以朝任一方向发展的东西。没有人被问过。原作已经发售。它是正典。社区知道它是什么样的。窗口已经关闭了。

这个想法就此消亡。不是因为它不够好。而是因为它好到足以重要，却迟到到了不可能实现的地步。你找到了一个变通方案。最终的作品是不错的——技术上扎实，专业上完成。但在你做出来的东西和你几乎做出来的东西之间，有一个由结构造成的差距，而没有人注意到它。

这种情况不断发生。没有人谈论它，因为已完成的作品不带着它差点成为什么的幽灵。

What You Never Knew Was Possible你从未知道有可能的事

There's a subtler version of this loss that goes in the other direction.

Most creative careers follow one of two tracks: you work on your own ideas, or you get commissioned to execute someone else's. Even within your own medium. The idea that a creator from a completely different medium might reshape how you approach your work — not by giving you a brief, but by being genuinely in dialogue with you about what the world should be — almost never happens.

So you never find out what it does to a composer when a game mechanic is being built alongside them rather than handed to them afterward. You never find out what a composer's early sketch does to a comic artist's sense of pacing. You never find out what that pacing reveals to a game designer about a system they thought was already finished.

The story that emerges from this process is unreproducible. It's shaped by the specific strengths and limitations of each medium, filtered through the specific people in that conversation. A different team with the same brief would produce a different world. The IP carries the fingerprint of how these people talked to each other. That is not a quality that can be replicated by adaptation, because adaptation started from a world that already existed before the conversation happened.

这种损失还有一个更微妙的版本，方向相反。

大多数创作生涯遵循两条轨迹之一：你在自己的想法上工作，或者你接受委托去执行别人的想法。即使在你自己的媒介内也是如此。来自完全不同媒介的创作者可能会重塑你处理自己作品的方式——不是通过给你一份简报，而是与你真正进行对话，探讨这个世界应该是什么——这几乎从未发生。

所以你永远不会知道，当一个游戏机制和作曲家一起被构建，而不是事后交给他们，这对作曲家来说意味着什么。你永远不会知道作曲家的早期草稿对漫画家的节奏感做了什么。你永远不会知道那种节奏对游戏设计师揭示了什么——关于他们以为已经解决了的系统。

从这个过程中浮现的故事是不可复制的。它被每种媒介的具体优势和局限所塑造，经过在场的特定创作者的过滤。换一个团队，同样的创作方向，会产出一个不同的世界。这个 IP 带着这些人彼此对话方式的指纹。这种品质无法通过改编来复制，因为改编是从一个在对话发生之前就已经存在的世界出发的。

What the Audience Finds观众发现的东西

The other half of this is what happens on the receiving end.

Every medium in this model stands completely on its own. The game is an excellent game. The comic is an excellent comic. The music holds without any other context. No medium requires knowledge of another to be understood or enjoyed. No homework.

But for the audience member who finishes the game and then discovers the comic, something different happens. They're not getting more of the same world — they're getting a new facet of the same world, made by someone who was in the original conversation. The panel pacing echoes something from the game they can't quite name. The song carries an emotional weight the story set up. Everything reveals more the more of it you engage with.

And all of it is original. All of it is canon simultaneously — not because a bible declared it so, but because it was all created at the same time, from the same root. The magic for the audience is discovering a whole new medium for something they already love. That discovery only happens when the mediums were made for each other from the start.

这件事的另一半，是发生在接收端的东西。

这个模式里的每一种媒介都完全能够独立成立。游戏是一款出色的游戏。漫画是一部出色的漫画。音乐在没有任何其他语境的情况下也成立。没有任何一种媒介需要了解另一种媒介才能被理解或享受。不需要做功课。

但对于那个玩完游戏后又发现了漫画的观众，某种不同的事情发生了。他们得到的不是更多相同的世界——他们得到的是同一个世界的一个新面向，由一个参与过原始对话的人创作。分格节奏呼应了游戏里某种他们说不清楚的东西。那首歌承载了故事埋下的情感重量。你越投入，整个作品就揭示越多。

而所有这些都是原创的。所有这些同时都是正典——不是因为某份圣经宣布了这一点，而是因为它们都在同一时间、从同一个根源被创作出来。对于观众来说，这种魔力在于：发现他们已经热爱的东西，拥有了一种全新的媒介形式。这种发现，只有在这些媒介从一开始就为彼此而生的时候，才会发生。

What co-design changes共同设计改变了什么 If the composer is in the room when the game mechanic is being designed — not scoring it afterward, but actually shaping what the world feels like alongside the designer — the mechanic and the music end up made from the same root. The composer's sense of what persistence sounds like can reshape the mechanic. The mechanic's feel can tell the composer something about what the music needs to carry that no brief could articulate. Both mediums are better for it. 如果作曲家在游戏机制被设计的时候就在场——不是事后来配乐，而是真正与设计师一起塑造这个世界的感觉——那么机制和音乐最终就会从同一个根源里被做出来。作曲家对"坚持听起来是什么样"的感知，可以重塑这个机制。机制的感觉可以告诉作曲家一些关于音乐需要承载什么的东西，这是任何简报都无法表达的。两种媒介都会因此变得更好。

Why It's a Structural Problem, Not a Skill Problem为什么这是一个结构性问题，而不是技能问题

The downstream model doesn't produce bad transmedia because the people are wrong. It produces adaptation because the people were brought in too late. By the time the composer arrives, the world's emotional logic is already encoded in the game's mechanics, its visual design, its narrative arc. The brief captures some of it. The rest is implicit. The composer is working from a translation of a translation.

No amount of skill fully compensates for not being in the original conversation. The work is always decoding what the anchor medium decided, not contributing to what the world actually is.

The model we're building starts with the opposite assumption: no medium is primary. No creator is downstream. The world doesn't exist before the conversation between mediums starts. The IP emerges from the dialogue. That's what makes it different — and what makes it something this structure makes possible for the first time.

下游模式之所以产生糟糕的跨媒介作品，不是因为人本身有问题。它产生改编，是因为人们被带进来的时间太晚了。等到作曲家到达的时候，这个世界的情感逻辑已经被编码进了游戏的机制、它的视觉设计、它的叙事弧线里。简报捕捉了其中的一些。其余的是隐性的。作曲家是在一个翻译的翻译版本上工作。

再多的技能也无法完全弥补没有参与最初的对话。这件作品永远在解码锚定媒介所决定的东西，而不是在贡献于这个世界真正是什么。

我们正在建立的模式从相反的假设出发：没有媒介是主导的。没有创作者是下游的。在媒介之间的对话开始之前，这个世界并不存在。IP 从对话中浮现。这就是让它与众不同的原因——也是让这个结构第一次使之成为可能的原因。
