03 · The IP Model

03 · IP 模型

IP is Experience. IP 即体验。

IP is not a product. It is the total experience an audience has when interacting with it, across every medium, every encounter, every conversation about it.

IP 不是一个产品。IP 是观众与它互动时所获得的全部体验，跨越每一个媒介、每一次相遇、每一段关于它的对话。

The Core Reframe核心的重新框定

A few examples to ground the term first. When we say "IP" in this document, we mean things like: Pokémon. Star Wars. Hello Kitty. Hatsune Miku. Marvel. Frozen. Genshin Impact. Totoro. Kpop Demon Hunters. Each one is something you can hold in your head as a single recognisable thing, a feeling, a world, a set of characters and atmospheres, even though the actual works that make them up are spread across games, films, music, comics, merchandise, concerts, fan art, and conversations on the internet. The IP is the recognisable thing in your head. The works are the containers it has appeared in.

IP is commonly understood as intellectual property in the legal sense, a set of owned characters, worlds, and narrative elements. This is a property lawyer's definition, and it is almost entirely useless for understanding how IP actually works from the audience's side.

From the audience's side, the IP is the experience of interacting with it. Not the characters in isolation, not the world, not the brand guidelines. The experience: the feeling you get playing it, watching it, listening to it, talking about it with someone who knows it, walking into a room and seeing someone else wearing it. Everything that touches a person in relation to this work is part of the IP in the only sense that matters, the experiential sense.

This distinction is not semantic. It has direct consequences for how you design, what decisions you make, and what you're actually trying to protect and build over time. A studio that thinks of IP as a set of owned assets will make different decisions at every juncture than a studio that thinks of IP as a set of experiences it's responsible for producing in its audience.

先用几个例子把这个词落地一下。我们在这份文档里说"IP"时，指的是这样的东西：宝可梦。星球大战。Hello Kitty。初音未来。漫威。冰雪奇缘。原神。龙猫。Kpop 猎魔女团。每一个都是你脑子里可以作为"一个能被认出来的东西"被持有的，一种感觉、一个世界、一组角色与氛围，哪怕真正构成它们的那些作品散布在游戏、电影、音乐、漫画、周边、演唱会、同人作品和网络上的对话里。IP 就是你脑子里那个"能被认出来的东西"。作品是它出现过的容器。

"IP" 通常被理解为法律意义上的知识产权，一组被持有的角色、世界与叙事元素。这是知识产权律师的定义，对于"IP 在观众那一侧如何真正运作"这个问题来说，几乎完全无用。

从观众那一侧来看，IP 是与它互动的体验。不是孤立的角色，不是世界，不是品牌规范。是体验：你玩它、看它、听它的感觉，你和懂它的人聊它的感觉，走进一个房间看到另一个人穿着相关周边的感觉。任何在这部作品周围触及到一个人的东西，都是 IP 的一部分，在唯一真正重要的意义上，也就是体验的意义上。

这个区分不是文字游戏。它对你如何设计、做什么样的决策、长期来说真正想要保护和构建的是什么，都有直接后果。一家把 IP 想成"一组被持有的资产"的工作室，与一家把 IP 想成"一组它有责任在观众身上产生的体验"的工作室，在每一个关键节点上做出的决策都会不一样。

1IP is Medium-AgnosticIP 与媒介无关

If IP is experience, then it isn't tied to any one medium, and audiences already know this without thinking about it. To a Pokémon fan, the Game Boy game, the animated series, the trading card, the plush toy, and the AR phone game are all just Pokémon. They don't sort their experiences by format. They're collecting encounters with the same world, the same creatures, the same feeling. The medium changes from one encounter to the next; the IP is what persists across all of them.

Star Wars works the same way. A lightsaber duel in a cinema, a LEGO X-Wing on a shelf, a theme park ride, a TV series about a bounty hunter: wildly different containers, and to a fan every single one of them is Star Wars.

如果 IP 即体验，那它就不与任何一种媒介绑定，而观众其实早就知道这件事，只是从未刻意去想。对一位宝可梦的粉丝来说，Game Boy 上的游戏、动画剧集、集换式卡片、毛绒玩具、在城市里走的 AR 手游，这些通通都只是宝可梦。他们不会按格式去分类自己的体验。他们是在收集与同一个世界、同一群生物、同一种感觉之间的一次次相遇。媒介从一次相遇到下一次都会变；IP 是贯穿所有这些相遇而留下来的那个东西。

《星球大战》也是一样。电影院里的光剑对决、书架上的乐高 X 翼战机、主题乐园的项目、关于赏金猎人的剧集：容器各不相同，但对一位粉丝来说，这每一个都是《星球大战》。

The consequence由此而来的结论 Most of the industry, when entering a new medium, defaults to one question: "how do we adapt our IP into this new format?" That question produces adaptation. The result feels like adaptation, because it is, and audiences can tell. We can do better. The better question is: "what experience of this IP is only possible in this medium, and how do we design that experience, so the audience encounters something native rather than translated?" Every medium has unique strengths nothing else has. Use them. Give the audience a piece of the IP they couldn't get anywhere else. 行业里大多数人，在进入一种新媒介时，默认问的是同一个问题："我们怎么把我们的 IP 改编进这个新格式里？"这个问题产出的就是"改编"。结果让人感觉像"改编"，因为它就是，而观众能感觉得到。我们可以做得更好。更好的问题是："这个 IP 有哪一种体验，是只有在这个媒介里才可能发生的？我们如何把那种体验设计出来，让观众接触到的是某种原生的东西，而不是被翻译出来的东西？"每一种媒介都有别的媒介所没有的独特优势。把它们用起来。给观众一份他们在任何其他地方都拿不到的、属于这个 IP 的东西。

2Discourse is Part of the Experience话语本身就是体验的一部分

Most people who will ever have an opinion about your IP will form that opinion without engaging with anything you made.

This sounds counterintuitive, but it is straightforwardly true. The first encounter most people have with any IP is not the IP itself, it is hearing about it. A friend's description. A recommendation algorithm. A meme. A review. Someone wearing a piece of merchandise on the subway. Before a person plays the game, watches the film, or opens the book, they have already formed a preliminary impression. And for a significant portion of the total potential audience, that impression is where the relationship ends.

This is not a failure mode to be corrected. It is simply how cultural objects work. Every book has far more people who have heard of it than people who have read it. The impression that forms from discourse is real: it shapes behaviour, it determines whether someone recommends it to others, it contributes to the cultural weight the IP carries.

The practical implication is that discourse is not marketing collateral external to the IP. Discourse is part of the IP experience itself. An IP that generates rich, warm, interesting discourse among its audience creates a positive impression for people who will never engage directly with any of its mediums. An IP that generates toxic, exhausting, or elitist discourse repels people long before they have a chance to encounter the work itself.

The creator's relationship to discourse is one of awareness and care, not management and control. You cannot and should not try to engineer the conversation. You can put things into the world that generate the kind of conversation you want to exist around it.

This logic extends to every deliberate choice the IP's creators make about how they exist in the world. How the collective communicates publicly, which venues host the music first, how the game studio responds to players, what the advertising looks like: none of these are external to the IP experience. They are part of it. When advertising looks and behaves like advertising, interrupting, selling, separate from the work, rather than like something designed from the IP's own internal logic, it is a fissure in the experience.

未来对你 IP 抱有意见的人当中，绝大多数都不曾真正接触过你做的任何东西，就已经形成了那个意见。

这听起来反直觉，但它直白地为真。大多数人与任何 IP 的"第一次相遇"不是 IP 本身，而是"听说"。一位朋友的描述。一段推荐算法。一个梗图。一篇评论。某个人在地铁上穿的周边。在一个人真正玩这款游戏、看这部电影、翻开这本书之前，他就已经形成了一个初步印象。而对于潜在受众中相当大的一部分人来说，那个印象就是关系的终点。

这不是一个需要被纠正的失败模式。这就是文化物件运作的方式。每一本书"听说过它的人"远远多过"读过它的人"。从话语场中形成的印象是真实的：它塑造行为，决定一个人是否会向别人推荐它，构成 IP 所携带的文化分量。

实际的含义是：话语并不是 IP 之外的营销附属品。话语本身就是 IP 体验的一部分。 一个能在自己受众间引发丰盛、温暖、有趣对话的 IP，对那些永远不会直接接触它任何一种媒介的人，也会留下一个正面印象。一个生产出有毒的、令人疲惫的、精英式排他话语的 IP，会在人们有机会接触作品本身之前就把他们推开。

创作者与话语的关系，是觉察与照看的关系，而不是管理与控制的关系。你不能、也不应该试图去工程化那一场对话。你可以做的，是把那种你希望"它周围会出现"的对话所需要的东西，放进世界里。

这个逻辑延伸到 IP 创作者关于"他们如何在世界中存在"所做的每一个有意识的选择。共创团体如何对外发言、音乐先在什么样的场所首发、游戏工作室如何回应玩家、广告长什么样：这些没有一项是 IP 体验之外的东西。它们都是 IP 体验的一部分。当广告看起来、行为表现也都"像广告"，打断、推销、与作品本身分离，而不是从 IP 自身的内在逻辑被设计出来的东西时，它就是体验里的一道裂缝。

The implication由此而来的含义 The collective itself is a character in the audience's relationship with the IP. The same emotional logic that governs the work should govern a reply to a fan on social media. What would an advertisement look like if it were designed by the same committee that designed the IP's foundation, using the same phenomenological logic? It probably wouldn't look like an advertisement at all. 共创团体本身，就是观众与 IP 之间关系里的一个角色。统御作品的那套情感逻辑，也应该统御对一位粉丝在社交媒体上的回复。如果一则广告，是由设计 IP 根基的那个班底，用同一套现象学逻辑去设计的，它会长什么样？它很可能根本不会看起来像一则广告。

3IP as a Living System作为有生命系统的 IP

IP is not a fixed product that gets distributed to an audience. It is a living thing that changes as people interact with it, and the audience is part of what it becomes.

  1. Among Us. The game released in 2018 and went nowhere. Then in 2020, during COVID lockdowns, a handful of Twitch streamers picked it up, millions joined, and overnight it became a cultural phenomenon. The game itself didn't change. The Among Us of 2020 is a fundamentally different IP than the Among Us of 2018. What changed was the living system around it.

  2. My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic. Made for young girls. Then the bronies, an adult male internet community, adopted it with genuine sincerity, built fan communities, created original work, and became so visible that they could not be ignored. They did not change the show. But they became part of what the IP meant in culture.

  3. Sanrio and Hello Kitty. Originally for young girls. As those girls grew up and kept their affection, Sanrio noticed, and instead of insisting the IP was "for kids," they leaned into adult consumers. The character didn't change, but who it was for and what it meant expanded because Sanrio responded to what the IP was actually becoming.

  4. Genshin Impact. HoYoverse built a permissive fan creation policy into the IP from the start, turning fans into unpaid marketers. The first anniversary tells the other side: when players felt the rewards were inadequate, they review-bombed unrelated HoYoverse apps and even Google Classroom. The same parasocial attachment that makes the IP powerful is what makes fans feel entitled to a say in how it's run. The attachment isn't incidental, it's the product. The living system here isn't organic, it's engineered.

  5. Arcane. The inverse: a creator choosing to evolve an IP toward an audience it hadn't yet reached. Riot made a show for people who hadn't yet found the game, prioritising emotional storytelling and visual craft over fan service. The gamble worked, and reframed what League of Legends was culturally. The conversion from Arcane viewer to LoL player was lower than expected, but that wasn't medium incompatibility. Arcane fans just didn't like LoL as a game.

  6. Winnie the Pooh in China. The most extreme illustration. Around 2013, internet users circulated comparisons of Winnie the Pooh and Xi Jinping. The memes spread. By 2018, China had effectively banned Winnie the Pooh content. Disney did nothing to create this association. The discourse created a meaning, that meaning became real enough that an entire government responded to it, and now Winnie the Pooh carries a layer of political connotation that will never fully separate from the character.

IP 不是一个被分发给观众的固定产品。它是一个会随着人们与它互动而改变的活物，而观众，就是它成为之物的一部分。

  1. 《Among Us》。 这款游戏 2018 年发售时几乎无人问津。到了 2020 年，疫情封锁期间，少数几位 Twitch 主播玩起了它，数百万人加入观看与游玩，一夜之间它成了一种文化现象。游戏本身没有变。2020 年的《Among Us》已经是一个与 2018 年的《Among Us》根本不同的 IP。改变的是它周围那个有生命的系统。

  2. 《My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic》。 本来是给小女孩的。然后"小马迷"（bronies），一群成年男性的网络社群，以真诚的姿态接纳了它，建立了庞大的粉丝社群，创作了原创作品，且影响力大到无法被忽视。他们没有改变这部动画。但他们成了这个 IP 在文化中"意味着什么"的一部分。

  3. 三丽鸥与 Hello Kitty。 最初也是给小女孩的。当那些小女孩长大、依然带着对她的情感时，三丽鸥注意到了，他们没有坚持说"这个 IP 是给小孩的"，而是顺势走向成年消费者。角色没有变，但她"为谁而存在"以及"她意味着什么"扩展了，因为三丽鸥回应了 IP 正在真实地变成的样子。

  4. 《原神》。米哈游从一开始就在 IP 里建立了宽容的同人创作政策，把粉丝变成了不付费的营销者。但第一次周年庆讲出了另一面：当玩家觉得奖励不足时，他们不仅恶评游戏，还波及到与原神无关的米哈游应用，甚至 Google Classroom。让这个 IP 如此强大的那种准社会投入，也让粉丝觉得自己有权干预运营。这种依附不是意外，它就是产品本身。这里的"有生命系统"不是有机长出来的，而是被工程化出来的。

  5. 《Arcane》（双城之战）。 反过来的方向：创作者主动选择把 IP 朝着它尚未触及的那一群观众进化。Riot 为那些"还没有找到这款游戏的人"做了一部剧，把情感叙事与视觉手艺放在粉丝服务之上。这个赌注成立了，重新定义了《英雄联盟》在文化层面上"是什么"。《Arcane》的观众转化为《英雄联盟》玩家的比例低于预期，但这不是"媒介不兼容"的问题。《Arcane》的粉丝只是不喜欢《英雄联盟》这款游戏。

  6. 小熊维尼与中国。 最极端的例子。大约从 2013 年开始，网民开始流传小熊维尼与习近平的对比图。梗图扩散。到 2018 年，中国已经实际上封禁了小熊维尼相关内容。迪士尼并没有做任何事情来制造这种联想。是话语创造了一个意义，而那个意义真实到足以让一整个政府以"真实威胁"的方式去回应它，于是小熊维尼现在带着一层永远无法完全从这个角色身上剥离的政治涵义。

Gardening, not engineering是园艺，不是工程 You plant things carefully and tend conditions, but you do not control what grows. You pay attention to what the living thing is actually becoming, and you respond to that honestly rather than insisting it conform to a plan written before the audience existed. An IP that refuses to evolve doesn't stay true to itself. It dies in place while the world moves on around it. 你小心地种下东西，照看周围的条件，但你不控制长出来的是什么。你留意这个活物正在真实地变成什么，并诚实地回应那个变化，而不是坚持要它符合一份在观众还不存在的时候就写好的计划。一个拒绝进化的 IP，并不会因此忠于自己。它只是停留在原地死去，而世界在它周围继续往前走。

True Transmedia真正的跨媒介

Henry Jenkins's definition of transmedia (2006) was a genuine attempt to describe what designing across mediums looks like. His core principles: each medium does what it does best; each entry is self-contained enough that a newcomer can start anywhere; each entry enriches the whole; the audience hunts and gathers across platforms.

The first principle is right. The other three need refinement.

Henry Jenkins 在 2006 年对"跨媒介"（transmedia）的定义，是一次真诚的尝试，去描述"跨多种媒介进行设计"看起来是什么样子。他的核心原则：每种媒介做它最擅长的事；每一项作品都足够自足，让新来者可以从任何地方开始；每一项作品都让整体更丰盛；观众跨平台"狩猎与采集"。

第一条原则是对的。后面三条需要被打磨。

The Matrix: a case study in the failure mode《黑客帝国》：一个失败模式的案例研究

The Matrix is the textbook example of Jenkins's model. The Wachowskis were explicit: the Animatrix shorts explained the machine war, Enter the Matrix bridged events between films, the comics filled in side characters. As a coordinated worldbuilding exercise, it was architecturally correct.

As a cultural phenomenon, it didn't work. Most audiences experienced only the films. The game received mediocre reviews. Casual viewers of Reloaded felt confused by character beats set up in the side material. The jigsaw model assumes an audience willing to hunt and gather fragments. Most audiences don't do this.

《黑客帝国》是 Jenkins 模型的教科书例子。沃卓斯基姐妹很明确：动画短片集《黑客帝国动画版》解释了机器战争的前史，《Enter the Matrix》游戏桥接了电影之间的事件，漫画补全了边缘角色。作为一次有协调的世界观构建，它在结构上是正确的。

但作为一种文化现象，它没奏效。大多数观众只接触了电影。游戏的评价也只是中等。普通观众看《重装上阵》时，对那些被埋在外围作品里铺垫过的角色脉络感到困惑。这种"拼图式"的模型假设观众愿意去狩猎、去收集碎片。大多数观众并不会这么做。

The Marvel comparison和漫威的对比

The MCU largely avoided this failure by keeping each film self-sufficient while rewarding cross-property attention. You can watch Iron Man without having seen The Incredible Hulk. Marvel's model is layered rather than jigsaw: each entry is complete, and additional viewing adds depth without being required.

漫威电影宇宙基本上避开了这种失败模式，它让每一部电影都足够自足，同时又奖励"跨作品关注"的观众。你可以不先看《无敌浩克》就直接看《钢铁侠》。漫威的模型是分层式的，而不是拼图式的：每一项作品都是完整的，而看更多只是增加深度，并不是必需。

Our model我们的模型

  1. Each medium does what it does best.每种媒介做它最擅长的事。 Medium choice is motivated by what that medium uniquely enables in the audience, not by distribution reach or format convention. 媒介的选择是由"那个媒介在观众身上独有地能产生什么"驱动的，而不是由分发触达或格式惯例驱动。
  2. Each entry is excellent in its own right.每一项作品本身就足够优秀。 Not merely "self-contained enough to avoid confusion," but genuinely complete and high-quality as a standalone experience worth engaging with on its own terms. 不只是"自足到不让人感到困惑"，而是作为一个独立体验，本身就完整、有品质，值得人们就它本身去接触。
  3. Additional mediums add layers, not missing pieces.追加的媒介加上的是"层"，不是"缺失的拼块"。 Each medium is a complete stratum; engaging with more adds depth, never fills in gaps that make someone without it feel lost. 每一种媒介都是一个完整的"层"；接触更多只是增加深度，绝不应该是去填上"没接触它的人就会迷路"那种缺口。
  4. Entry is permission-based.入场是基于"许可"的。 The audience self-selects their medium and their level of engagement. No path is canonical. Depth is offered, not required. 观众自己选择从哪一种媒介进入、投入到什么程度。没有任何一条路径是"正统"的。深度是被提供的，不是被要求的。
  5. Discourse is part of the IP.话语就是 IP 的一部分。 The social field around the work is constitutive of how the IP is experienced, not external marketing. 作品周围的社交场域，是 IP 如何被体验的"构成性的一部分"，不是外部的营销。
  6. The mediums are co-designed, not adapted.这些媒介是共同设计的，不是被改编的。 The IP world is built through cross-medium dialogue from the start. No medium is primary. The world emerges from the conversation between mediums, not from one medium's logic being translated into others. IP 世界从一开始就通过跨媒介对话被一同建造。没有一种媒介是"主导"。世界从媒介之间的对话中浮现，而不是某一种媒介的逻辑被翻译进其他媒介里。

Transmedia by Design vs by Adaptation"按设计"做 vs "靠改编"做的跨媒介

By Adaptation靠改编

Starts from a single-medium work. The game was designed for game mechanics; the manga adaptation translates game logic into panel flow; the soundtrack is composed around a finished product. Each subsequent medium is downstream. The result always feels like adaptation, because it is.

从一个单一媒介作品起步。游戏是按游戏机制被设计的；漫画改编把游戏逻辑翻译进分镜流；原声带是围绕一个已经完成的产品被作曲的。每一个后来的媒介都是下游。结果永远让人感觉像"改编"，因为它本来就是。

By Design按设计

Starts from the IP's truth. A composer's ideas about what persistence sounds like feed back into how game mechanics are designed. A manga artist's sense of how silence lands reshapes how a live event uses quiet moments. The creative process itself is intermedial from the start. No medium is primary. No medium is downstream.

从 IP 的"真相"起步。作曲人对"坚持听起来是什么样"的探索，会反过来影响游戏机制如何被设计。漫画家对"沉默如何落下"的感知，会重塑现场活动如何使用安静时刻。创作过程本身从一开始就是"跨媒介式的"。没有一种媒介是主导。没有一种媒介是下游。

The result is a world whose internal coherence cannot be achieved by any other means. Not because it was enforced from the top, but because the mediums shaped each other throughout development. The coherence is structural, emergent from the process.

This maps to a deeper distinction: additive vs multiplicative transmedia. The entire industry, including the best transmedia consultancies, thinks in additive terms. The world exists, each medium adds content to it. In multiplicative transmedia, the game mechanic changes how you read the novel. The ambient score recontextualises the exhibition. The visual pattern contains a key that unlocks something in the music. Not Easter eggs, structural interdependence. The meaning of any single medium is incomplete not because content is missing, but because meaning is only fully formed in the interaction between mediums.

结果是一个内在连贯性无法用任何其他方式实现的世界。不是因为有人在顶上强行执行，而是因为各种媒介在整个开发过程中相互塑形。这种连贯是结构性的，是从过程本身中涌现出来的。

这背后还映射着一个更深的区分：加法式 vs 乘法式的跨媒介。整个行业，包括最好的跨媒介顾问公司，都是用"加法"的方式在思考的。世界已经存在，每一种媒介向它里面"加"内容。而在乘法式的跨媒介里，游戏机制改变你读那部小说的方式。环境配乐重新语境化了那个展览。视觉图案里包含一把钥匙，能解锁音乐里的某种东西。不是彩蛋，是结构性的相互依赖。任何单一媒介的意义之所以不完整，不是因为有内容缺失，而是因为意义只有在媒介之间的互动中才会完整成形。

Engineering Cross-Discovery把"互相发现"工程化

The model this is closest to in practice: Kpop Demon Hunters (2025). The film was excellent. The music was better. Together, they each elevated the other to heights neither could have reached alone. People who found the music through TikTok went on to watch the film. People who watched the film went on to find the music.

Cross-discovery requires two things to be true simultaneously: each medium must have its own independent acquisition channel, and the mediums must be deeply linked. Neither alone is sufficient.

The formula: deep interlinking + separate acquisition channels = cross-discovery. The test for whether deep interlinking was achieved is simple: are the two mediums equated in the audience's memory? "Let It Go" is Frozen. "Golden" is Kpop Demon Hunters. That equivalence is what deep interlinking produces.

The counterexample: Transformers and Linkin Park. "New Divide" was written specifically for Revenge of the Fallen, originally going to be named "Megatron." This looks like co-design. But it wasn't. The song was made for Transformers; Transformers did not change for the song. The causality runs one way. This is the distinction between commissioned and co-designed. Commissioned means one medium is made to serve another. Co-designed means both mediums have genuinely changed each other.

这个模型在实践中最接近的对象是《Kpop 猎魔女团》（2025）。电影很出色。音乐更出色。两者放在一起时，彼此把对方抬升到任何一方单独都无法达到的高度。在 TikTok 上发现这首歌的人会去看电影。看了电影的人会去找音乐。

"互相发现"需要两件事同时为真：每一种媒介都必须有它自己独立的拉新渠道，而媒介之间又必须有深度连结。两者缺一不可。

公式：深度互联 + 各自独立的拉新渠道 = 互相发现。检验"是否达成了深度互联"的方法很简单：这两种媒介在观众的记忆里是不是被等同起来的？《Let It Go》就是《冰雪奇缘》。《Golden》就是《Kpop 猎魔女团》。这种"等同"，正是深度互联的产物。

反例：《变形金刚》与林肯公园。《New Divide》是专门为《变形金刚 2：堕落者的复仇》写的，最初甚至打算命名为《Megatron》。从外面看，这像是"共同设计"。但其实不是。歌是为《变形金刚》而做；《变形金刚》并没有为这首歌而改变。因果只朝一个方向走。这就是受委托与共同设计的区别。受委托意味着一种媒介为服务另一种媒介而被制作。共同设计意味着两种媒介都真正地相互改变了对方。
