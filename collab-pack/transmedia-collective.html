04 · The Collaboration Model

04 · 协作模式

The Transmedia Collective. 跨媒介共创团体。

You've seen what this model makes possible and why nothing like it exists yet. This is what you'd actually be joining: what the collective is, why it's structured the way it is, what it means for your medium, and how the work happens.

你已经看到了这个模式能带来什么，以及为什么类似的东西还不存在。这是你真正会加入的东西：共创团体是什么，为什么以这种方式构建，它对你的媒介意味着什么，以及工作如何进行。

This structure is still a draft. What follows is what we've implemented as our best attempt to make collaboration as fair as possible for everyone, but the details are open for renegotiation with the group of collaborators. If something here doesn't fit how you'd want to work, that's a conversation we want to have.

这个结构仍是草稿。以下内容是我们尽力让协作对所有人尽可能公平的最佳尝试，但细节可以与协作者群体重新协商。如果这里有什么不符合你想要的工作方式，这是我们希望进行的对话。

The structural advantage: Every major player in this space (studios, publishers, established creators) has spent years building a vertical identity. They are a game studio. They are a record label. Co-equal authorship across mediums would require them to become something their own ecosystem doesn't have a word for. They won't do it. This model has to be built by creators who are building their identity now, not because they're lacking something, but because they're the only people who can build the collective into their foundation rather than bolt it onto something already finished.

结构性优势：这个领域中的每一个主要参与者（工作室、发行商、成熟创作者）都花了多年时间建立垂直身份。他们就是游戏工作室。他们就是唱片公司。跨媒介的平等共同著作权，将要求他们成为他们自己的生态系统里没有词汇来描述的东西。他们不会这么做。这个模型必须由正在现在构建身份的创作者来建立——不是因为他们缺少什么，而是因为他们是唯一能够将共创团体建入基础的人，而不是将它螺栓到已经完成的东西上。

What You're Joining

你将加入什么

The transmedia collective is not a studio. It is not a publisher. It is not a work-for-hire arrangement.

It is a creative collective of experts in their own mediums, co-creating an IP world through cross-medium dialogue, with shared ownership of what they build together.

The founding structure is the facilitator's contribution. The world is co-created together.

Play Instigator's role is to have developed this model: the structure, the philosophy, the collaboration framework, the financial architecture. Beyond that, our ongoing role is to facilitate the collaboration itself: making sure it actually happens, and that the IP that comes out of it is coherent. The world the collective builds belongs to everyone who builds it. You're not joining someone else's project. You're authoring something that doesn't exist yet.

You're still doing what you'd do anyway, but with a seat in other mediums' design rooms.

A composer will still be making music. A game designer will still be making games. What changes is the creative context, the potential scale, and the ownership. One of the genuinely rare things this collective offers is a seat in the design discussions of other mediums, especially around how your medium might collaborate with theirs. This almost never happens in the entertainment landscape. When crossovers happen, it's usually one side commissioning work from the other. Co-designing something from the ground up across mediums is something almost no one gets to do.

The downside is real and should be named.

Shared ownership means less control than making your own thing. Deferred revenue is a consequence of the limited resources we have starting out, but it's no different from what any indie studio faces when it tries to do its own thing without knowing whether it'll succeed. We are indies. We started this in order to do our own things. We know the risk and we are willing to take it. The collective is just a way of pooling our risk together so we have a shot at making something bigger than any one of us could alone. This is an experimental model. The first time it's being done.

跨媒介共创团体不是工作室。不是发行商。不是雇佣关系。

它是一个由各自媒介专家组成的创意集体，通过跨媒介对话共同创造IP世界，并共同拥有他们共同构建的东西。

创始结构是促进者的贡献。世界由大家共同创造。

Play Instigator的角色是开发了这个模型：结构、哲学、协作框架、财务架构。除此之外，我们持续的角色是促进协作本身：确保它真正发生，并且由此产生的IP是连贯的。集体构建的世界属于构建它的每一个人。你不是在加入别人的项目。你是在创作一个尚不存在的东西。

你仍然在做你本来就会做的事，但在其他媒介的设计室里有了一个席位。

作曲家仍然会创作音乐。游戏设计师仍然会制作游戏。改变的是创意背景、潜在规模和所有权。这个集体提供的真正罕见的东西之一，是在其他媒介设计讨论中的席位，尤其是关于你的媒介如何与他们的媒介协作。这在娱乐领域几乎从未发生过。当跨界合作发生时，通常是一方委托另一方工作。从头开始跨媒介共同设计某样东西，几乎没有人有机会做到。

不利之处是真实的，应该被点明。

共同所有权意味着比自己做事拥有更少的控制权。延迟收入是我们起步时有限资源的结果，但这与任何独立工作室在不知道是否会成功的情况下尝试做自己的事情没有什么不同。我们就是独立者。我们开始这件事是为了做我们自己的东西。我们知道风险，我们愿意承担它。集体只是一种将我们的风险集中在一起的方式，让我们有机会做出任何一个人单独无法完成的更大的东西。这是一个实验性模型。是第一次被做到。

What This Model Makes Possible

这个模型让什么成为可能

The traditional path means competing with everyone in your medium. The noise is enormous. Most games, albums, comics, and films are never discovered by the audience that would love them.

This model means there is no competition. No one else is building transmedia IP this way. The structural difficulty of doing it is exactly what makes it a moat. The harder it is to build, the harder it is to replicate.

  1. Your medium is primary, not derivative. Your medium would never be "the game based on the anime" or "the soundtrack to the film." It is an original expression of a world you helped author, built simultaneously with every other medium. There is no hierarchy of canon.

  2. Audience reach compounds across the collective. Each member brings their own audience, even a small one. Because those audiences don't fully overlap, a release goes out to the union of everyone's reach rather than one person's alone. Starting out, the combined reach may be modest, but the structural compounding is real, and it scales as each member grows.

  3. Your medium gets pushed forward. A game designer gets asked "what does persistence feel like mechanically?" by a composer who has already answered the same question musically. That conversation changes what you make, not toward compromise, but toward capability you wouldn't have reached alone.

  4. You propose what your medium makes. We don't write a brief and hand it to you. No one in the collective knows your medium better than you do, and we won't pretend to. What only a song can do, what only a comic panel can do, what only a physical object can do, that's the expertise you bring. Each seat in this pack proposes a starting frame for the conversation, but the deliverable, what scale it lives at, what shape it takes, how it leverages your medium's unique strengths, comes from you. The collective is a thinking partner in your medium, not a prescription.

传统路径意味着与你媒介中的所有人竞争。噪音是巨大的。大多数游戏、专辑、漫画和电影从未被真正会喜爱它们的观众发现。

这个模型意味着没有竞争。没有其他人以这种方式构建跨媒介IP。做到这件事的结构性难度，正是它成为护城河的原因。越难构建，就越难复制。

  1. 你的媒介是主要的，而不是衍生的。你的媒介永远不会是"基于动漫的游戏"或"电影的原声带"。它是你帮助创作的世界的原创表达，与所有其他媒介同时构建。没有正典的层级。

  2. 观众触达在集体中复合增长。每个成员都带来自己的观众，哪怕是小的。因为这些观众并不完全重叠，一次发行面向的是所有人触达的并集，而不是一个人的。起步时，合并触达可能是适度的，但结构性复合是真实的，随着每个成员的成长而扩展。

  3. 你的媒介被推向前进。一位游戏设计师被一位已经用音乐回答了同样问题的作曲家问道："坚持在机制上是什么感觉？"那次对话改变了你的创作，不是走向妥协，而是走向你独自无法达到的能力。

  4. 你提议你的媒介创作什么。我们不会写一份提案然后交给你。集体中没有人比你更了解你的媒介，我们不会假装了解。只有歌曲能做什么，只有漫画格能做什么，只有物理对象能做什么——这是你带来的专业知识。这个包中的每个席位提议一个对话的起始框架，但交付物——它存在的规模、它呈现的形状、它如何利用你媒介的独特优势——来自你。集体是你媒介中的思考伙伴，而不是处方。

How the IP World Gets Built

IP世界如何被构建

The key principle: no medium is downstream. The world emerges from the dialogue between mediums, not from one medium's logic being translated into others.

Conventional production: Vertical. A creative director specifies what a section needs to do ("this moment should feel tense"), and each collaborator executes that specification in their medium. The direction flows one way.

Cross-medium integration: Horizontal. Collaborators are in genuine dialogue about what the world is. A composer's exploration of a theme can reshape how a game mechanic is designed. A manga artist's panel structure can suggest a new interaction model. Each retains full creative control over their own medium.

In practice: the music sketch inspires the comic artist, whose storyboard inspires the spatial designer, whose floor plan surfaces a question the narrative writer hadn't considered. This is what "transmedia by design, not by adaptation" looks like in a room.

核心原则：没有媒介处于下游。世界从媒介之间的对话中涌现，而不是从一个媒介的逻辑被翻译到其他媒介中。

传统制作：垂直的。创意总监规定某个部分需要做什么（"这个时刻应该感觉紧张"），每个协作者在自己的媒介中执行该规范。方向单向流动。

跨媒介整合：水平的。协作者对世界是什么进行真正的对话。作曲家对主题的探索可以重塑游戏机制的设计方式。漫画家的分镜结构可以提出新的交互模型。每个人在自己的媒介中保留完全的创意控制权。

在实践中：音乐草稿启发漫画家，漫画家的分镜启发空间设计师，空间设计师的平面图浮现出叙事作家尚未考虑的问题。这就是"跨媒介设计，而非改编"在一个房间里的样子。

Because the world is built from medium-native communication (a composer's sketch, a storyboard, a floor plan), the foundation has the specific texture of how these people talked to each other. A different founding team with the same brief would produce a different world. The IP is irreproducible because the process that made it is irreproducible. That is an ontological moat.

Canon is structural, not designated. If a game mechanic was co-designed with the composer and the narrative writer in the same room, the mechanic is canon in the same way the music is canon. The cross-medium connections are load-bearing, not decorative.

因为世界是从媒介本土的沟通方式（作曲家的草稿、分镜、平面图）构建的，基础具有这些人彼此交谈方式的特定质感。一个不同的创始团队，拿着相同的提案，会产生一个不同的世界。这个IP是不可复制的，因为创造它的过程是不可复制的。这是一个本体论上的护城河。

正典是结构性的，而非被指定的。如果一个游戏机制是与作曲家和叙事作家在同一个房间里共同设计的，那么这个机制就是正典，就像音乐就是正典一样。跨媒介的联系是承重的，而非装饰性的。

Why We Think Audiences Don't Follow Stories Across Mediums

为什么我们认为观众不会跨媒介追随故事

A note on the analysis from here on. Much of what follows, why we think most transmedia hasn't worked, why we believe co-design is a better bet, is our reading. We don't have proof. We're a small group of creators betting on a hypothesis we find compelling, partly because no one else seems to be testing it this way. Treat the analysis as our reasoning for trying this, not as established truth.

The pattern we keep seeing in transmedia experiments that didn't land (Marvel's homework problem, Star Wars hiding plot beats inside Fortnite, Kingdom Hearts requiring you to have played seven consoles' worth of side games) is what looks to us like a shared assumption: that audiences are loyal to content. Our bet is that they are more loyal to medium and consumption appetite.

Gamers want more games. Film viewers want more films. Comic readers want more comics. Music listeners want more music. There is overlap, but in our reading it's much smaller than transmedia narratives suggest.

When a piece of content requires consumption of another medium to be understood, the addressable audience plausibly collapses to the intersection of medium audiences, not the union. Each "AND" multiplies the audience down. That logic, if it holds, is why something like The Rise of Skywalker's Fortnite-only Palpatine setup landed with confused audiences.

Every model in this pack is built around respecting this. Each medium in the collective must hold completely on its own, for an audience that may never engage with any other medium in the IP. No homework. No prerequisites. Depth offered, not demanded. The cross-medium fan is rewarded with extra meaning; the single-medium fan is not punished with missing pieces.

关于以下分析的说明。以下大部分内容——为什么我们认为大多数跨媒介实验没有奏效，为什么我们相信共同设计是更好的赌注——是我们的解读。我们没有证明。我们是一小群创作者，押注于我们认为有说服力的假设，部分原因是似乎没有人以这种方式测试它。将这个分析视为我们尝试这件事的理由，而不是既定的真理。

我们在未能落地的跨媒介实验中不断看到的模式（漫威的"功课"问题、星球大战将剧情节点隐藏在堡垒之夜中、王国之心要求你玩七台主机的支线游戏）在我们看来像是一个共同的假设：观众对内容忠诚。我们的赌注是，他们对媒介和消费偏好更为忠诚。

玩家想要更多游戏。电影观众想要更多电影。漫画读者想要更多漫画。音乐听众想要更多音乐。存在重叠，但在我们的解读中，它比跨媒介叙事所暗示的要小得多。

当一个内容要求消费另一种媒介才能被理解时，可寻址观众就可能坍塌为媒介观众的交集，而不是并集。每一个"AND"都将观众向下相乘。如果这个逻辑成立，就解释了为什么《天行者崛起》的堡垒之夜专属帕尔帕廷设定让观众感到困惑。

这个包中的每个模型都是围绕尊重这一点构建的。集体中的每个媒介必须对可能永远不会接触IP中任何其他媒介的观众完全独立成立。没有功课。没有前提。提供深度，而不是要求。跨媒介粉丝被额外的意义所奖励；单媒介粉丝不会因为缺失的部分而受到惩罚。

The third loyalty type, and why we're trying something different

第三种忠诚类型，以及我们为什么尝试不同的东西

Genshin Impact appears to break this rule. Tens of millions of people follow Genshin characters across the game, manga, music, concerts, and fan art, some without playing the game at all. Our reading: Genshin isn't a game that also has a fandom, it's structured more like a fandom machine that uses a game as its primary delivery vehicle. The characters are the product. Every medium exists to deepen attachment to those characters, not to be excellent in its own right. The gacha system is the monetisation of that attachment.

This works. It works at an extraordinary scale. We don't want to dismiss it. But it seems to produce a specific kind of output: creative work designed to serve the fandom, not the medium. The game isn't popular only because it's a brilliant game. The music seems to exist to reinforce character attachment more than to stand as a remarkable piece of music. Most creative decisions read, to us, as subordinate to the fandom and its monetisation.

For a fan artist, participating in that ecosystem can be genuinely enjoyable. For a creator who wants to make something excellent in their medium (a composer writing music that stands as music, a comic artist making panels that work as panels), the fandom-first model can become a ceiling rather than a floor. The medium risks becoming fan service.

What we're trying to build is different. We want mediums that are each genuinely excellent: work that stands on its own terms, without needing a fandom to justify it. The fandom that comes from that, if it comes, is a different kind: one that attaches to the quality of what was made, not just to characters as objects of parasocial investment. That is a harder thing to build, and we may be wrong that it's even possible at the scale we're hoping for. We think it's worth trying.

原神似乎打破了这个规则。数千万人跨游戏、漫画、音乐、演唱会和同人作品追随原神角色，其中一些人甚至根本没有玩过游戏。我们的解读：原神不是一个也有粉丝群的游戏，它更像是一台以游戏为主要传递媒介的粉丝群机器。角色才是产品。每种媒介的存在都是为了加深对这些角色的依附，而不是以自身为优秀。抽卡系统是对这种依附的变现。

这是有效的。在非凡的规模上有效。我们不想否定它。但它似乎产生了一种特定类型的输出：为粉丝群服务而非为媒介服务的创意工作。这个游戏之所以受欢迎，不仅仅是因为它是一款出色的游戏。音乐的存在似乎更多是为了强化角色依附，而不是作为一件出色的音乐作品独立成立。大多数创意决定在我们看来都从属于粉丝群及其变现。

对于一个粉丝艺术家来说，参与那个生态系统可以是真正令人享受的。对于一个想要在自己的媒介中创作出色作品的创作者（一位写出能作为音乐独立成立的音乐的作曲家，一位创作能作为格子独立成立的漫画家），粉丝群优先的模型可能成为天花板而非地板。媒介有沦为粉丝服务的风险。

我们试图构建的是不同的东西。我们想要各自真正出色的媒介：在自己的条件下独立成立的作品，无需粉丝群来证明其合理性。由此而来的粉丝群，如果会来的话，是一种不同类型的：依附于所创作之物的质量，而不仅仅依附于角色作为准社会投资的对象。这是一件更难构建的事情，我们可能在我们希望的规模上是否甚至可能时是错误的。我们认为值得尝试。

Case Study: The Japanese Media Mix

案例研究：日本媒体混合模式

The closest existing structural parallel to the collective is the Japanese media mix, the production committee (seisaku iinkai) model behind nearly every major anime IP.

What we share with it:

  • Multi-medium from origin: multiple mediums planned simultaneously at the start
  • Each medium consumable on its own: no homework required across mediums
  • World + characters as the connection, not plot dependency
  • Merchandise as a real revenue medium (Gunpla outearns Gundam anime; Pokémon trading cards outearn the games and films combined)

Where it stops, and where we begin:

A production committee is a coalition of companies, not creators. Each takes rights to a different medium and a percentage stake in IP revenue. They financialise and distribute. They do not co-author the world. The IP itself almost always exists before the committee, usually as a successful manga or light novel.

The mediums in a media mix are produced in parallel, not in dialogue. Each committee partner optimises their medium for their own ROI. There is coordination on world facts, character designs, and release windows, but the mediums do not actively reshape each other during creation.

The collective begins exactly where the committee stops. The seed of the IP is created through cross-medium dialogue from day one. The members are individual creators, not corporate rights-holders. The mediums actively reshape each other in development. And unlike the committee (which gates membership to roughly thirty major Japanese companies from traditional commercial mediums), the collective is open to any medium where a creator genuinely shapes how an IP is experienced, including mediums the committee has never had a seat for.

与集体最接近的现有结构平行是日本的媒体混合，即几乎所有主要动漫IP背后的制作委员会（制作委员会）模型。

我们与它的共同点：

  • 从起源就是多媒介：在开始时同时规划多种媒介
  • 每种媒介都可以独立消费：跨媒介不需要功课
  • 世界观+角色作为联系，而非情节依赖
  • 周边商品作为真正的收入媒介（高达模型的收入超过高达动漫；宝可梦集换式卡牌的收入超过游戏和电影的总和）

它停止的地方，以及我们开始的地方：

制作委员会是公司的联合，而非创作者。每家公司获得不同媒介的权利和IP收入的百分比股份。他们进行金融化和分发。他们不共同创作世界。IP本身几乎总是在委员会之前就存在，通常是作为成功的漫画或轻小说。

媒体混合中的各媒介是并行制作的，而不是在对话中制作的。每个委员会合伙人都在为自己的ROI优化自己的媒介。在世界事实、角色设计和发行窗口上有协调，但这些媒介在创作过程中并不主动相互重塑。

集体正是从委员会停止的地方开始。IP的种子从第一天起就通过跨媒介对话创造。成员是独立创作者，而非企业版权持有人。这些媒介在开发中主动相互重塑。与委员会（其成员资格仅限于来自传统商业媒介的约三十家主要日本公司）不同，集体向任何真正塑造观众体验IP方式的媒介开放，包括委员会从未有席位的媒介。

A note on Genshin Impact

关于原神的说明

Genshin Impact uses media mix aesthetics (character-centric, merchandise, manga, concerts, anime) but its structure looks, to us, different from the production committee. Genshin is game-first: other mediums seem to exist mostly to serve the game and funnel audiences back to its gacha system. That puts it structurally closer to the Disney anchor model than to the committee model, despite the surface resemblance. Genshin may also represent the anchor model's ceiling: when audiences attach to characters as objects of parasocial investment rather than to mediums, the anchor medium can become less important than the fandom itself. The game is brilliant, but a significant part of its success seems to come from how well the fandom has been engineered; every medium, including the game, functions as a vehicle for that attachment. The production committee doesn't have an anchor medium; it has parallel mediums each owned by a different company. Genshin's mediums look, from where we stand, more downstream of the fandom than parallel to each other. The comparison table below shows where our model is trying to depart from both.

原神使用媒体混合美学（以角色为中心、周边商品、漫画、演唱会、动漫），但其结构在我们看来与制作委员会不同。原神是游戏优先的：其他媒介似乎主要存在于服务游戏并将观众引回其抽卡系统。这使它在结构上更接近于迪士尼锚点模型，而非委员会模型，尽管表面上有相似之处。原神也可能代表了锚点模型的天花板：当观众依附于角色作为准社会投资的对象而非依附于媒介时，锚点媒介可能变得比粉丝群本身更不重要。这个游戏很出色，但其成功的很大一部分似乎来自于粉丝群被工程化得多好；每种媒介，包括游戏，都作为那种依附的媒介而发挥作用。制作委员会没有锚点媒介；它拥有各由不同公司持有的并行媒介。从我们的角度来看，原神的各媒介看起来更多是粉丝群的下游，而非彼此并行的。下面的比较表展示了我们的模型试图从两者中脱离的地方。

The audience reward: Easter eggs, not homework

观众奖励：彩蛋，而非功课

In the Japanese model, a fan who consumes the manga, the anime, and the figure line gets the same world delivered three times. They get more coverage of the same world, but no extra meaning from consuming more.

In the collective model, a fan who picks up the comic after playing the game notices that the panel timings echo combat tempo. The colour palette in act three resolves a question the game's environmental design left open. A song lyric becomes a cosplay motif becomes a recurring visual in the comic. The cross-medium fan finds more, not just more of the same. The fan who consumed only one medium still has a complete experience (no homework was required), and the cross-medium fan is rewarded with deeper meaning, not punished with prerequisites.

在日本模型中，一个消费了漫画、动漫和手办系列的粉丝，得到的是同一个世界被传递三次。他们得到了同一个世界更多的覆盖，但从消费更多中没有获得额外的意义。

在共创团体模型中，一个玩完游戏后又拿起漫画的粉丝注意到分镜时序呼应了战斗节奏。第三幕的色彩调板解答了游戏环境设计留下的疑问。一句歌词变成cosplay主题，变成漫画中反复出现的视觉元素。跨媒介粉丝发现的是更多，而不仅仅是更多相同的东西。只消费了一种媒介的粉丝仍然有完整的体验（不需要功课），而跨媒介粉丝被更深的意义所奖励，而不是被前提条件所惩罚。

What This Means for Your Medium

这对你的媒介意味着什么

Most creators have worked in a room where the IP was already decided. You got the brief. You executed it well. The work was good. But the question "what should this world actually feel like?" was answered before you arrived.

The collective is built around a different structure. Your medium (what it uniquely makes possible, how it processes time, silence, narrative, space) is in the room before the world is fixed. Not to decorate what someone else decided. To shape what gets decided.

Traditionally, there are two paths to having that say. The first: climb until you're the creative director. Then you call the shots, but you've spent years executing other people's briefs to get there. The second: do everything yourself. Full creative control, but you're alone, and the scale of what you can build is limited to what one person can carry.

The collective is a third path. Build something larger than you could alone, with people who are each striving in their own medium, and have a genuine say in the entirety of what gets made, not just your corner of it.

大多数创作者都曾在IP已经决定的房间里工作。你拿到了提案。你很好地执行了它。作品是好的。但"这个世界实际上应该是什么感觉？"这个问题在你到来之前就已经被回答了。

集体是围绕不同的结构构建的。你的媒介（它独特地使什么成为可能，它如何处理时间、沉默、叙事、空间）在世界被固定之前就在房间里。不是为了装饰别人决定的东西。而是为了塑造被决定的东西。

传统上，拥有那种发言权有两条路径。第一条：爬升直到你成为创意总监。然后你说了算，但你花了多年执行别人的提案才到达那里。第二条：自己做所有事情。完全的创意控制，但你是孤独的，而且你能构建的规模受限于一个人能承载的东西。

集体是第三条路径。与每个人都在自己的媒介中努力的人们一起构建比你单独能完成的更大的东西，并在所做之物的整体上拥有真正的发言权，而不仅仅是你的角落。

The how is only half of it. Co-designing a world from the ground up isn't just a process question. It's a values question. You're not executing someone else's brief. You're authoring what this world is, what it carries, what it does to the people who encounter it. That requires alignment on something more fundamental than mechanics: what kinds of worlds are worth building? The next page is that conversation.

方式只是其中一半。从零开始共同设计一个世界不仅仅是一个过程问题。这是一个价值观问题。你不是在执行别人的提案。你是在创作这个世界是什么，它承载什么，它对遇到它的人做什么。这需要在比机制更根本的层面上保持一致：值得构建什么样的世界？下一页就是那个对话。
