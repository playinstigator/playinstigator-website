07 · 一起工作

The Deal. 合作结构。

What joining the collective actually looks like. The structure is still a draft. It's our best attempt to be fair, and it's designed to be renegotiated with the first cohort. Here it is in plain terms.

加入共创团体实际上是什么样子。这个结构仍然是草稿。这是我们尽力公平的最佳尝试，它被设计为与第一批成员重新协商。以下是简明的说法。

The Honest Version First

先说诚实的版本

There is no upfront payment. Everyone, including the person running this, is investing their time and craft in an IP that doesn't yet have revenue. The trade is equity in the thing you help build: if it works, you share in what it earns, in proportion to what you put in and how it performs.

This is not the right arrangement for everyone. If you need income now from the work you're putting in, this isn't that. If you need creative control over a solo project, this isn't that either. What it is: a way of making something genuinely ambitious alongside people who are each pushing their own medium, where the work you put in earns you a share of what it generates. If the IP earns, you earn from it.

没有预付款。所有人，包括运营这件事的人，都在将时间和技艺投入到一个尚无收入的IP中。交换是你帮助构建的东西的权益：如果成功了，你按照你投入的比例和它的表现来分享它所赚的。

这不是适合所有人的安排。如果你现在需要从你投入的工作中获得收入，这不是那样的。如果你需要对一个独立项目拥有创意控制权，这也不是。它是什么：一种与各自都在推进自己媒介的人们一起创作真正有抱负的东西的方式，你投入的工作为你赚得它所产生的一份。如果IP赚钱，你从中赚钱。

How the Money Works

钱如何运作

There are a few ways revenue flows to the people in the collective.

  1. Your medium's direct revenue — the game, the music, the comic, the merch each generate their own revenue stream. The person who runs that medium earns from it directly, with a recoup period to recover production costs first. The split at steady state reflects how much of the value came from your craft versus the IP equity built collectively.
  2. Cross-medium contribution — if you meaningfully shaped someone else's work from the outside, you earn a share of that medium's revenue too. The more you're genuinely in dialogue with the other mediums, the more revenue streams you participate in.
  3. The collective pool — revenue that belongs to the IP as a whole rather than any single medium flows here. It's distributed monthly based on how active each contributor has been, weighted toward people who've been in longer.

The structure is designed around one principle: the people who do the work share in what it earns, in proportion to what they contributed and for how long. Early contributors are recognised for the risk they took when the pool was small. Sustained presence counts more than a single deliverable.

For every mechanism, split, and edge case: Revenue Sharing Structure. Read it before you agree to anything, and raise it if something doesn't sit right.

有几种方式让收入流向集体中的人。

  1. 你的媒介的直接收入 — 游戏、音乐、漫画、周边各自产生自己的收入流。运营该媒介的人直接从中赚钱，首先有一个收回制作成本的回收期。稳定状态下的分成反映了多少价值来自你的技艺，相对于集体共同构建的IP权益。
  2. 跨媒介贡献 — 如果你从外部有意义地塑造了别人的工作，你也能从那个媒介的收入中赚得一份。你越真正与其他媒介保持对话，你参与的收入流就越多。
  3. 集体池 — 属于整体IP而非任何单一媒介的收入流入这里。每月根据每位贡献者的活跃程度分配，权重倾向于参与时间更长的人。

这个结构围绕一个原则设计：做工作的人按其贡献和持续时间的比例分享它所赚的。早期贡献者因为在池子还小的时候承担的风险而得到认可。持续的存在比单一的可交付成果更重要。

对于每一个机制、分成和边缘情况：收入分享结构。在你同意任何事情之前阅读它，如果有什么感觉不对，请提出来。

What the Working Arrangement Looks Like

工作安排是什么样子

In-person co-design sessions: roughly one session a week, one day on weekends, location in Singapore. These are the sessions where the mediums actually talk to each other, where the composer and the game designer and the visual artist are in the same room working out how their work shapes each other's.

Everything else, your own medium's production work, is done on your own time, at your own pace, against milestones agreed with the collective.

The sessions are not compulsory for every piece of work you do. They are where the co-design happens. If you can't be physically present in Singapore for them, this particular arrangement won't work. The whole point of building from dialogue requires that you're actually in the dialogue.

线下共同设计会议：大约每周一次，周末一天，地点在新加坡。这些是媒介真正相互对话的会议，作曲家、游戏设计师和视觉艺术家在同一个房间里，研究他们的工作如何塑造彼此的工作。

其他一切——你自己媒介的制作工作——按照你自己的时间、自己的节奏，依照与集体商定的里程碑来完成。

这些会议对你做的每一件事并非强制性的。它们是共同设计发生的地方。如果你不能亲自出现在新加坡参加这些会议，这种特定的安排就不会起作用。从对话中构建的全部意义需要你真正参与对话。

Sessions are recorded, and that matters twice

会议被录制，这有两重意义

Every co-design session is recorded, audio or video. This serves two distinct purposes and both are serious.

For accountability: The earned contribution process, deciding who from outside a medium meaningfully shaped a piece of work, depends on accurate records of what was actually said and when. Memory months after the fact is unreliable and leads to disputes. The recording is the evidence base. If you shaped something, it needs to be on record at the time. Not reconstructed later.

For content: The recordings are raw material for behind-the-scenes documentation: process clips, devlogs, social media content, portfolio pieces. A collective building a transmedia IP across multiple creators is itself a story worth telling. The process of making the work is content for each medium's audience. You're not just building the IP; you're documenting how it gets built, and that documentation has value of its own.

每次共同设计会议都被录制，音频或视频。这服务于两个截然不同的目的，两者都是严肃的。

为了问责：赚得贡献的流程——决定谁从媒介外部有意义地塑造了一件作品——依赖于对实际说了什么、何时说的的准确记录。几个月后凭记忆是不可靠的，会导致争议。录制是证据基础。如果你塑造了某样东西，它需要在当时有记录。不是事后重构。

为了内容：录制是幕后记录的原始素材：过程片段、开发日志、社交媒体内容、作品集素材。一个由多位创作者共同构建跨媒介IP的集体本身就是一个值得讲述的故事。制作工作的过程就是每个媒介观众的内容。你不只是在构建IP；你在记录它是如何被构建的，而那份记录本身就有价值。

What You're Agreeing To

你同意的是什么

When you join the first cohort, you're agreeing to:

  • Participate in the co-design sessions for the current phase (through end of 2026 for the first round)
  • Deliver your medium's contribution to the agreed milestones
  • Be part of the ongoing conversation about what the world is becoming
  • Renegotiate the details of this structure with the rest of the first cohort before it's finalised

What you're not agreeing to: an ironclad contract you had no say in, a fixed schedule that ignores your life, a creative brief you have to execute without input, or a structure that can't flex when the situation warrants.

当你加入第一批成员时，你同意：

  • 参与当前阶段的共同设计会议（第一轮到2026年底）
  • 将你的媒介贡献交付到商定的里程碑
  • 成为关于世界正在成为什么的持续对话的一部分
  • 与其余第一批成员重新协商这个结构的细节，然后再最终确定

你没有同意的是：一份你没有发言权的铁板钉钉合同、一个忽视你生活的固定时间表、一份你必须无需投入而执行的创意提案，或者一个不能在情况需要时灵活调整的结构。

What We're Looking For

我们在寻找什么

Not the most experienced person. Someone who cares about the question more than the credentials.

Specifically for the first round: a composer or musician who wants to co-design a sonic world, not score a finished product. A visual artist (illustrator, comic artist, or similar) who wants to build the visual language of a world from scratch. Possibly a toy/product designer who sees the physical form as an extension of the world, not a merchandise spin-off.

If your medium isn't listed here and you think it belongs in this collective, the answer isn't no. It's a conversation.

不是最有经验的人。是比凭证更关心这个问题的人。

具体对于第一轮：一位想要共同设计声音世界的作曲家或音乐人，而不是为完成的产品配乐。一位想要从零开始构建世界视觉语言的视觉艺术家（插画师、漫画家或类似的）。可能是一位将实体形态视为世界延伸而非周边衍生品的玩具/产品设计师。

如果你的媒介没有在这里列出，而你认为它属于这个集体，答案不是不。而是一次对话。

The structure is open Everything in this document — percentages, durations, mechanisms — is a working draft. It's what we've worked out as our best attempt to be fair. When the first cohort comes together, we'll go through it together, question the parts that don't fit, and agree on the final version collectively. The structure is on the table, not behind glass. 结构是开放的 这份文件中的一切——百分比、持续时间、机制——都是工作草稿。这是我们尽力公平所做出的最佳尝试。当第一批成员聚集在一起时，我们将一起过一遍，质疑不合适的部分，并集体商定最终版本。结构摆在桌上，而不是在玻璃后面。
