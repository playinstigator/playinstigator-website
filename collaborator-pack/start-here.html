For our composer · Ritual & Ruin

致我们的作曲人 · Ritual & Ruin

A different kind of seat for a composer. 一个为作曲人留下的、不一样的座位。

Most game composers get handed a finished product and asked to score it. We're inviting you to do something else: shape the world's audio and write the song that defines the IP — alongside the people designing the game, before the game is finished.

大多数游戏作曲人拿到的是一个已完成的产品，然后被要求给它配乐。我们邀请你做的是另一件事：塑造这个世界的音频同时写一首定义这个 IP 的歌——与正在设计这款游戏的人并肩工作，在游戏完成之前。

What you'd actually make 你实际上会做的东西

This is a single combined role, not two separate gigs. The same person owns the entire sonic world of the game — music, SFX, ambience — and writes the standalone artefact that defines the IP outside it: at minimum a single defining song, or a small album if the work calls for it. The standalone needs vocals; if you don't sing or write vocals yourself, that's a conversation we'd have with you — figuring out together whether to look for a vocalist jointly or whether you'd bring in someone you already trust. We work it out with you, not around you. The two halves of the role are designed to feed each other — sonic decisions in one shape the other.

这是一个合在一起的单一角色，不是两份分开的工作。同一个人持有这款游戏整个声音世界——音乐、音效、环境音——同时写出那件在游戏之外定义这个 IP 的独立成立作品：最少是一首"定义性的单曲"，如果工作本身需要，也可以是一张小型专辑。独立作品必须有人声；如果你自己不演唱、也不写人声部分，那是我们想和你一起聊的事——一起去看是要共同寻找一位人声合作者，还是由你把自己已经信任的人带进来。这件事我们和你一起想，而不是绕过你来做。这两半角色是被设计成互相喂养的——其中一边的音色决定，会塑形另一边。

What this will actually require of you 这件事实际上会向你要求什么

We need to be honest about what saying yes to this means in practice. Not to scare anyone off — but because the wrong fit hurts both sides, and the conversation goes better when we're upfront.

我们需要诚实地告诉你：对这件事说"是"，在实际操作中意味着什么。不是要吓跑任何人——而是因为错配对双方都是伤害，而事先把话说清楚，对话会进行得更好。

Your evenings and weekends你的晚上和周末

Until the IP is producing revenue stable enough to sustain us, this is a side commitment for everyone — including the founder. What we need from you is not a specific number of hours; it's that your weekends and evenings are mostly available for this work. What you do during the day is your own business. If you have a day job or other gigs, that's fine. If you're already working full-time in your medium, the cleanest fix is to shift your week — for example, take Monday and Tuesday off and work Wednesday through Sunday — so you're free when the rest of us are.

在 IP 产生的收入稳定到足以维持我们的生活之前，这件事对每个人来说都是一种"额外的承诺"——包括创始人在内。我们需要你给出的，不是一个具体的小时数，而是你的周末和晚上大部分时间可以为这件事腾出来。你白天做什么，是你自己的事。你有正职、有别的接单工作，都没问题。如果你已经在自己的媒介里全职工作了，最干净的处理方式是把你的"一周"挪一下——比如周一、周二休假，周三到周日工作——这样你就能在我们其他人有空的时候也有空。

Weekends are where the real work gets done: ideation sessions, deeper creative conversations, the integrated work between mediums. Evenings and late nights are mostly for shorter check-ins, small pieces of collaboration, and async work — you don't get much done at 11 PM on a Tuesday after a full day, and we won't pretend otherwise.

周末才是真正能把事情往前推的地方：构思讨论、更深入的创作对话、跨媒介之间需要整合的工作。晚上与深夜，主要用来做较短的 check-in、小块的协作片段、以及异步的工作——星期二晚上 11 点忙了一整天之后能做的事其实不多，我们不会假装不是这样。

If your weekends and evenings are already fully spoken for and not movable, this isn't going to work.

如果你的周末和晚上都已经被排满了、又挪不动，那这件事就行不通。

Tolerance for creative friction对创作摩擦的容忍

Doing co-design across mediums is harder than doing your own thing. You will disagree with people. People will disagree with you. There will be stretches where it feels like nothing is converging, where every direction feels wrong, where the work seems further from done than it was a month ago. That's not the model failing — that's what creative rigor at this scale actually looks like. Anyone who needs every conversation to end in alignment will burn out fast.

在多个媒介之间共同设计，比单独做自己的东西难得多。你会和别人意见相反，别人也会和你意见相反。会有那么几段时间，感觉一切都没有在收拢、每一个方向都不对、整件事看起来比一个月前离完成更远。这不是模型在失败——这就是这种规模上的创作严谨性真实的样子。任何需要"每一次对话都以达成一致告终"的人，会很快把自己烧光。

Real dedication to your craft对自己手艺真正的投入

What gets you through the friction is not love for the project — projects come and go. It's love for your craft itself, deep enough that the difficulty of doing it well is something you've already accepted as the price of doing it at all. The collective amplifies that — it doesn't replace it.

撑你穿过这些摩擦的，不是对这个项目的爱——项目来来去去。是你对自己手艺本身的爱，深到"把它做好的难度"早已被你接受为"做这件事的代价"。共创团体放大这种投入——它不替代它。

Self-driven craft growth自我驱动的手艺成长

The collective is built around cross-medium dialogue and cross-medium innovation. Within your own medium, you are the only expert. No one else on the team will mentor you in your craft — if you're the music person, nobody here will teach you to write better music; that growth has to come from you. We can challenge you about how your music meets the world, the story, the visuals. We can't teach you the craft itself. That responsibility is entirely yours.

这个共创团体是围绕"跨媒介对话"与"跨媒介创新"建造起来的。在你自己那门媒介之内，你是唯一的那个专家。团队里没有任何其他人能在你的手艺上指导你——如果你是做音乐的那个人，这里没有人会教你怎么把音乐写得更好；那种成长必须由你自己驱动。我们可以就"你的音乐如何与这个世界、这个故事、这些画面相遇"来挑战你。我们没办法教你这门手艺本身。那份责任完全由你承担。

Extraordinary work is extraordinarily difficult. That's not a warning, it's the whole point. If this were easy, someone would already have done it. 非凡的工作就是非凡地难。这不是一句警告——这就是事情的全部要点。如果这事很简单，早就有人做过了。

Other mediums we're looking to bring in 我们正在寻找的其他媒介

This isn't a composer-and-founder two-person operation. For Ritual & Ruin's IP to work the way we've described, the collective needs more than just game and audio. The other seats we're actively planning to fill, alongside yours:

这不是一个"作曲人 + 创始人"的二人小团队。要让 Ritual & Ruin 的 IP 按我们所描述的方式运作，这个团体需要的不只是游戏和音频。除你之外，我们正在主动寻找填补的其他座位：

  • Merch and product design — high-margin physical product lines (plushies, posters, prints, apparel) that monetise the IP across the audiences each medium brings in. An IP-Leveraged medium in the revenue model.
  • 周边与产品设计——高利润率的实体产品线（毛绒玩具、海报、印刷品、服装），借助每一种媒介带来的受众把 IP 变现。在收益模型里属于"IP 借力"型媒介。
  • A standalone narrative medium, likely a comic — a parallel-medium artefact that proves the transmedia model in practice, not just on paper. Same world, different entry point, ideally cross-discovered with the game.
  • 一种独立成立的叙事媒介，很可能是漫画——一件平行媒介的作品，用以在实际操作中（而不是仅仅在纸面上）验证跨媒介模型。同一个世界，不同的入口，理想状态下与游戏之间能形成"互相发现"。
  • Marketing-as-medium — short-form animated content, devlogs, behind-the-scenes documentation of the collective's process. These are Experience Mediums in the revenue model: they add to the IP rather than just point at it.
  • "营销即媒介"——短篇动画内容、开发日志、共创团体内部过程的幕后纪录。它们在收益模型里属于"体验型媒介"：它们为 IP 本身添加东西，而不只是指向 IP。

You wouldn't be filling any of these — they're separate seats. But they're being planned in parallel with yours, and the cross-medium dialogue you'd be part of is a real one with real other people around the table.

你不会被要求去填补其中任何一个座位——它们都是独立的位置。但它们与你的座位是并行规划的，你将参与的那场跨媒介对话，是一场桌边真的有其他人在场的对话。

Why we're doing it this way 我们为什么这样做

If those two briefs make sense to you and you want to understand the bigger model behind them, the next two documents are the answer. The audio role isn't isolated — it's one seat in a transmedia collective that's being built around the same principles.

如果那两份简报你看得通、并且想理解它们背后更大的那套模型，接下来这两份文档就是答案。音频这个角色并不是孤立的——它是一个跨媒介共创团体里的一个座位，整个团体都是围绕同一套原则建造起来的。

03 · The IP Model03 · IP 模型

IP is ExperienceIP 即体验

IP is not a set of owned assets. It is the total experience an audience has across every medium, every encounter, every conversation. Why music written with a game (not for it) becomes part of how the IP exists.

IP 不是一组被持有的资产。IP 是观众在每一个媒介、每一次相遇、每一段对话中所获得的总体体验。为什么"与游戏一起写出来的音乐"（而不是"为了游戏写的音乐"）会成为 IP 存在方式的一部分。
