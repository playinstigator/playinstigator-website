For our comic artist · Ritual & Ruin

致我们的漫画作者 · Ritual & Ruin

A different kind of seat for a comic artist. 一个为漫画作者留下的、不一样的座位。

Most comic artists working in games get handed a finished world and asked to adapt it. We're inviting you to do something else: build the visual language of this world from scratch, alongside the people designing the game, before the visual canon exists.

大多数参与游戏项目的漫画作者都拿到了一个完成的世界，被要求改编它。我们邀请你做的是另一件事：从零开始构建这个世界的视觉语言，与正在设计游戏的人并肩工作，在视觉正典尚未存在之前。

This is a sample seat. The version we share to show what a comic artist's involvement could look like. The actual scope, responsibilities, and deliverables get shaped by who you are and what you bring. Treat it as a starting point for a conversation, not a fixed brief.

这是一个示例座位。 我们分享这个版本，是为了让你看到漫画作者的参与可能是什么样子。实际的范围、职责和交付物，会根据你是谁、你能带来什么来一起塑造。把它当作一段对话的起点，而不是一份被固定下来的简报。

The Comic漫画

The comic is your medium. A standalone artefact set in the world of Ritual & Ruin, the same world as the game, but a completely different entry point. The lore, the characters, the moments the game can't show. The creature before it completes a ritual. The civilisation that built the altars. The lab that watches and doesn't intervene. The things that happen between matches.

漫画是你的媒介。一件独立作品，置于 Ritual & Ruin 的世界中，与游戏同处一个世界，却是一个完全不同的切入口。那些游戏无法呈现的传说、角色与时刻。生物在完成仪式之前的样子。建造祭坛的文明。那个冷眼旁观、从不介入的实验室。发生在每一场对局之间的事情。

The comic can be found first by readers who have never heard of the game, and pull them into the IP from an entirely different angle. It doesn't require the game to make sense. It doesn't retell the game. It stands on its own and makes the world larger.

漫画可以首先被从未听说过这款游戏的读者发现，从一个完全不同的角度将他们带入这个 IP。它不需要游戏来让自己变得有意义。它不是在复述游戏。它自立门户，让这个世界变得更大。

What it covers, the story, the characters, the structure, is not decided. That's yours to shape, in conversation with the collective. The world has a theme, a mechanic, and a starting fiction. The comic's specific entry point into that world is an open question that belongs to you.

它涵盖什么——故事、角色、结构——都尚未决定。这是你与共创团体共同塑造的空间。这个世界有它的主题、机制和初始虚构。漫画切入这个世界的具体方式，是一个属于你的开放问题。

What visual narrative uniquely affords视觉叙事独有的能力

These are the things only your medium offers. No song, no game, no physical product can deliver them. They are not constraints; they are why the comic is in this collective at all. How to leverage them is yours to propose. You're the expert in your craft. No one else in the collective knows your medium better than you do, and no one else can tell you how to use it.

这些是只有你的媒介才能提供的东西。没有任何歌曲、游戏或实体产品能做到。它们不是限制，而是漫画能够进入这个共创团体的根本原因。如何善加利用，由你来提议。你是这门手艺的专家。共创团体里没有人比你更了解你的媒介，也没有人能告诉你该如何使用它。

Reader-controlled pacing. Unlike a song or a game cinematic, the reader sets the pace. They can dwell on a panel for thirty seconds or scan a page in three. They can re-read a panel before going on. They can flip back. The comic is the only medium in the collective where the audience holds the time.

读者掌控的节奏。不像一首歌或游戏过场动画，读者自己决定阅读节奏。他们可以在一个画格上停留三十秒，也可以三秒内扫过一整页。他们可以在继续之前重读一个画格，也可以往回翻阅。漫画是共创团体中唯一一个由观众掌握时间的媒介。

Time in space. Multiple moments can be visible simultaneously on a spread. Before, during, and after exist as one image. A panel sequence collapses an arc into a single field of vision. No other medium presents time this way.

空间中的时间。多个时刻可以在一个跨页上同时呈现。之前、当时、之后共存于一张图像之中。一组画格序列将一段弧线压缩进单一的视野。没有其他媒介以这种方式呈现时间。

Silence and the gutter. The space between panels is where the reader does the work, inferring the action that connects them. A held beat with no sound, no motion, no text is a real tool in your medium. Other mediums can imply absence; only the comic uses it as a structural element.

沉默与格间。画格之间的空白，是读者主动补完的地方，他们会推断连接两者的动作。一个没有声音、没有动作、没有文字的停顿拍，是你媒介中真实存在的工具。其他媒介可以暗示缺席，但只有漫画将它用作结构性元素。

Page composition as meta-rhythm. A page turn, a splash page, a wide horizontal panel are rhythmic devices that operate at a scale above the panel. The reader experiences pacing at two levels simultaneously: panel-by-panel and page-by-page.

页面构成作为元节奏。翻页、整页满版、宽幅横向画格，都是在画格尺度之上运作的节奏装置。读者同时在两个层次上体验节奏：逐格感受，以及逐页感受。

Word and image as one unit. Speech, sound effects, and captions integrate as visual elements with the art. The same line of dialogue can be visually loud or quiet depending on how it's drawn. No medium binds verbal and visual signal as tightly as yours.

文字与图像合为一体。对白、音效和说明文字作为视觉元素与画面融合。同一句台词，根据绘制方式的不同，可以在视觉上喧嚣，也可以低沉。没有任何媒介能像你的一样，将语言信号与视觉信号绑定得如此紧密。

Permanence and re-reading. A reader returns to a specific panel days later. A comic can be re-read non-linearly, flipped through to find one moment. The artefact persists in physical form; the engagement does too.

持久性与重读。读者在几天后回到某个特定的画格。漫画可以非线性地重读，随意翻阅去寻找某一个瞬间。作品以实体形式持续存在；投入其中的情感同样如此。

The question isn't only "what should the comic look like?" It's "what does the reader do inside this comic that they can't do inside any other medium in the collective?" That judgement is what you bring.

问题不只是"漫画应该看起来是什么样子"，而是"读者在这部漫画中能做到什么，是在共创团体的任何其他媒介中都做不到的"。这个判断，就是你带来的东西。

Your role in shaping the visual canon你在塑造视觉正典中的角色

Visual decisions made here ripple outward. How a creature holds its weight. What angle it looks most unsettling from. How its form changes across the three stages: base, empowered, terminal. How decay warps its silhouette. How the world looks when it isn't a game arena.

在这里做出的视觉决策会向外扩散。生物如何承载自身的重量。从哪个角度看它最令人不安。它的形态如何跨越三个阶段演变：基础、强化、终末。腐化如何扭曲它的轮廓。当世界不再是游戏竞技场时，它看起来是什么样子。

Those decisions don't belong to any one medium. The comic artist brings what works on a page. The toy designer brings what resolves in three dimensions at small scale. The game brings what reads as a sprite or at a distance. The visual canon is what emerges from that dialogue, not what any one medium imposes on the others. What the comic contributes is being the first place those decisions exist as actual committed art, which gives it significant early weight in the conversation.

这些决策不属于任何单一媒介。漫画作者带来的是在页面上行得通的东西。玩具设计师带来的是在小尺寸三维空间中能够成立的东西。游戏带来的是作为像素精灵或在远距离下依然清晰可辨的东西。视觉正典是从这场对话中涌现出来的，而不是由任何一种媒介强加给其他媒介的。漫画的贡献在于，它是那些决策第一次以真正落定的艺术形式存在的地方，这赋予了它在对话中举足轻重的早期分量。

The first time you and the composer and the game are in the room together deciding what a creature looks and sounds like in its base form. That's a founding decision. It becomes the reference everything else is built from.

第一次，你与作曲人和游戏设计师同处一室，共同决定一个生物在基础形态下的外观和声音。那是一个奠基性的决策。它会成为所有其他一切据以建立的参照。

What success looks like成功是什么样子

For someone who found the comic first: They encountered the world through your visual storytelling, your creatures, your architecture, your sense of how this civilisation felt before and after it broke. The game deepens it when they find it. The world they thought they understood gets a new dimension. They go back to your pages and read them differently.

对于先发现漫画的人：他们通过你的视觉叙事邂逅了这个世界——你的生物，你的建筑，你对这个文明在崩坏前后感受的把握。当他们找到游戏时，游戏加深了这一切。他们以为自己已经理解的世界，多了一个新的维度。他们翻回你的页面，用不同的眼光重读它们。

For someone who found the game first: Your comic shows them what the game couldn't, the spaces between matches, the lab through a different lens, the creature as a subject rather than a game piece. Or even a completely different time and place that was affected by what happened in the labs. The world feels larger. The IP has weight it didn't have before.

对于先发现游戏的人：你的漫画向他们展示了游戏无法呈现的东西——对局之间的空隙，从不同视角看到的实验室，作为主体而非游戏棋子的生物。或者，甚至是一个完全不同的时间与地点，那里的一切都被实验室中发生的事情所波及。世界感觉变大了。这个 IP 有了此前所没有的分量。

For the IP: The comic and the game are equated in readers' minds. The visual language the collective built together has a consistent, recognisable identity across every medium it appears in.

对于这个 IP：漫画与游戏在读者心中被画上等号。共创团体共同建立的视觉语言，在它所出现的每一种媒介中，都拥有一致且可辨识的身份认同。

Why This Approach Works为什么这种方法行得通

Most licensed game comics are downstream. They're made after the game exists, adapting a visual world designed for a screen, for an audience that already found it through the game. They feel like supplements, interesting for fans, invisible to everyone else. They have no acquisition channel of their own and create no cross-discovery.

大多数授权游戏漫画都处于下游位置。它们在游戏已经存在之后才被制作出来，将一个为屏幕设计的视觉世界改编给那些已经通过游戏找到它的受众。它们感觉像是补充材料，对粉丝有吸引力，对其他所有人却是隐形的。它们没有自己的获客渠道，也不创造任何跨渠道发现。

The standalone comic approach inverts this. A comic that doesn't require the game to make sense has its own discovery infrastructure: Webtoon, manga platforms, comic shelves, Instagram discovery, convention floor previews. A reader who finds it that way encounters the IP through your visual storytelling, not through a Steam page. That path doesn't require any marketing. The platform and format do the work once the comic is there.

独立作品漫画的方式将这一切颠倒过来。一部不需要游戏才能讲通的漫画，有其自身的发现基础设施：Webtoon、漫画平台、实体书架、Instagram 发现页、展会现场预览。以这种方式找到它的读者，通过你的视觉叙事邂逅这个 IP，而不是通过一个 Steam 页面。这条路径不需要任何营销。一旦漫画在那里，平台和形式就会自己完成工作。

The cross-discovery formula is the same across every medium in this collective: deep interlinking + separate acquisition channels. A chapter that ends on the creature's terminal transformation creates emotional incompleteness, the reader senses there's a fuller arc they're not seeing yet. That gap pulls them toward the game. The game enriches the comic for them when they find it. Neither is upstream.

跨渠道发现的公式在共创团体的每一种媒介中都是一样的：深度相互关联 + 独立的获客渠道。一个以生物的终末形态变化作为结尾的章节，制造了情感上的未竟感——读者意识到有一段更完整的弧线他们还没有看到。这种缺口将他们拉向游戏。当他们找到游戏时，游戏为他们丰富了漫画。两者都不在上游。

What the comic's own acquisition channels look like漫画自身的获客渠道是什么样的

A chapter serialised on Webtoon reaches readers who have never heard of an indie game. A spread shared on Instagram or Tumblr gets discovered through visual aesthetics alone: the dark kawaii palette, the creature design, the panel composition. A physical volume at a convention sits on a table next to other things in its genre, and someone picks it up because of the cover. None of these entry points require the game to exist first.

在 Webtoon 上连载的一个章节，能够触达那些从未听说过任何独立游戏的读者。分享在 Instagram 或 Tumblr 上的一张跨页图，仅凭视觉美学就能被发现：暗系卡哇伊色调、生物设计、画格构图。摆在展会桌上、旁边放着同类型作品的一本实体书，会因为封面而让人拿起它。这些切入口，没有一个需要游戏先行存在。

Social clip logic applies here too. A single panel of the terminal transformation, shared without context, is its own artefact. Either it makes someone stop and look, or it doesn't. If it does, they want to know what they're looking at. That curiosity is the acquisition channel.

社交媒体短片的逻辑在这里同样适用。一张呈现终末形态变化的单格画，不带任何背景地分享出去，本身就是一件独立作品。要么它让人停下来看，要么不会。如果会，他们就想知道自己在看什么。那份好奇心，就是获客渠道。

The failure mode: adaptation vs co-design失败模式：改编 vs 共同设计

The failure mode is a comic that summarises what the game already shows, a visual recap of events the player already played through, written for fans who want more of the same. That's not a standalone artefact. It has nothing to offer someone who hasn't played the game, and nothing new to offer someone who has.

失败的模式是一部总结游戏已经呈现内容的漫画——对玩家已经经历过的事件进行视觉回顾，写给那些想要"更多同款"的粉丝。那不是独立作品。对于没玩过游戏的人，它毫无可提供的；对于玩过的人，它也没有任何新东西。

The question isn't "what happens in the game?" It's "what happens in the world that the game can't show?"

问题不是"游戏里发生了什么"，而是"这个世界里发生了什么，是游戏无法呈现的"。

Integration with the Game与游戏的整合

The game currently has placeholder creature visuals. The creature designs you develop for the comic are the most concrete answer to what these creatures actually look like, and that answer feeds directly back into the game's art direction. Your panels become reference material. Your page compositions surface spatial questions about how the world is experienced. The game isn't upstream of the comic. They're in dialogue.

游戏目前使用的是占位的生物视觉素材。你为漫画开发的生物设计，是对这些生物究竟长什么样的最具体的回答，而这个回答会直接反馈进游戏的美术方向。你的画格成为参考素材。你的页面构图让人们开始思考关于世界如何被体验的空间性问题。游戏不在漫画的上游。它们处于对话之中。

That dialogue goes both ways. A visual solution you find for depicting transformation across a page spread may suggest a way the evolution system communicates its stages. The way you solve negative space in a tense sequence may change how the game designer thinks about stillness in the game's pacing. The game designer is watching what you make.

这场对话是双向的。你在跨页上呈现变化时找到的视觉解法，可能会启发进化系统传达阶段变化的方式。你处理紧张序列中负空间的方式，可能会改变游戏设计师对游戏节奏中静止感的理解。游戏设计师在看你做出来的东西。

The visual canon isn't a separate deliverable. It's the shared language every part of the collective works in, built through the dialogue between mediums that each have different visual requirements. Your panels are one of the primary inputs into that conversation.

视觉正典不是一个独立的交付物。它是共创团体每个部分都在使用的共同语言，通过各自有着不同视觉要求的媒介之间的对话建立起来。你的画格是这场对话的主要输入之一。

The following are integration ideas: starting points for what the comic and game dialogue could produce. None of this is committed scope. The actual direction gets shaped together.

以下是一些整合构想：漫画与游戏的对话可能产出的起点。这些都不是已承诺的范围。实际方向将由大家共同塑造。

Panel borders as stage indicator画格边框作为阶段指示

Panel border weight can carry the same information the game communicates through creature scale. Thin borders for base-stage creatures, thicker for powered, borderless or full-bleed for cosmic horror. This isn't decoration: it's the same communication system expressed in the comic's grammar. Requires the comic artist and game designer to agree on how stage transition reads spatially in both mediums at the same time.

画格边框的粗细可以承载游戏通过生物体型所传达的相同信息。基础阶段的生物用细边框，强化阶段用更粗的边框，宇宙恐怖阶段则无边框或满版出血。这不是装饰：它是同一套传达系统，用漫画的语法表达出来。这需要漫画作者与游戏设计师同时就阶段过渡在两种媒介中的空间呈现方式达成一致。

Release cadence as narrative clock发布节奏作为叙事时钟

Each game update that introduces a new mechanic (Stage 2 evolution unlocking, a new ritual type) has a corresponding comic chapter showing that moment from the creatures' perspective. Not an explanation of it. The experiential reality it implies. Game design decisions about unlock timing directly constrain chapter content, and chapter release dates constrain when mechanics can be introduced. Neither is upstream.

每次引入新机制的游戏更新（第二阶段进化解锁、新仪式类型），都有一个对应的漫画章节，从生物的视角展示那个时刻。不是对它的解释，而是它所蕴含的体验现实。游戏设计中关于解锁时机的决策直接限制了章节内容，而章节发布日期则限制了机制何时可以被引入。两者都不在上游。

UI as shared visual languageUI 作为共享的视觉语言

What the game renders as a UI element, the comic renders as an in-world visual. Blood meters in the game become literal blood trails pooling toward altars in panels. Progress bars become physical accumulation. This mapping has to be agreed early, because it changes how the game's UI is designed: it needs to carry visual metaphor weight, not just function. Both teams decide the mapping together before either builds it.

游戏将某些东西呈现为 UI 元素，漫画则将其呈现为世界内部的视觉。游戏中的血量条，在画格中变成真实的血流汇聚向祭坛。进度条变成实体的积累。这个映射必须尽早达成共识，因为它会改变游戏 UI 的设计方式：它需要承载视觉隐喻的重量，而不仅仅是功能。两个团队在各自动工之前，共同决定这个映射关系。

Integration with the Music与音乐的整合

The following are integration ideas: starting points for what the comic and music dialogue could produce. None of this is committed scope. The actual direction gets shaped together.

以下是一些整合构想：漫画与音乐的对话可能产出的起点。这些都不是已承诺的范围。实际方向将由大家共同塑造。

Panel density as dynamic map画格密度作为动态映射

Panel count per page functions like rhythmic density: more panels compress time into faster tempo, fewer panels hold a sustained note or silence. A practical co-design arrangement: the composer receives the comic's page layouts before scoring a chapter's corresponding track, and uses panel count per spread as a guide for rhythmic density. Silent panels map directly to rests or held tones. Both creators agree on this grammar once; it runs as a consistent rule through the project.

每页画格数量的功能类似于节奏密度：更多的画格将时间压缩成更快的节奏，更少的画格则保持一个延续的音符或静默。一种实际可行的共同设计安排：作曲人在为某章节的对应配乐谱曲之前，先收到漫画的页面布局，并将每个跨页的画格数量作为节奏密度的参考。静默画格直接映射为休止或延音。两位创作者一次性就这套语法达成共识；它作为贯穿整个项目的一致规则运行。

Colour palette as harmonic register色彩调色板作为和声音域

The palette arc across the comic, warm and pastel in base-stage chapters, shifting toward cold, desaturated, high-contrast as the horror deepens, can map to harmonic register shifts in the music. Major-adjacent modes early; dissonant, modal, or microtonal textures late. If the comic artist defines the palette at each chapter and the composer responds to it, neither is upstream. Both mediums are responding to the same emotional temperature.

漫画整体的色调弧线——基础阶段章节中的温暖粉彩，随着恐怖加深向冷色调、低饱和、高对比度转变——可以映射到音乐中的和声音域转变。早期使用接近大调的音阶；后期使用不协和、调式或微分音质感。如果漫画作者在每个章节定义调色板，而作曲人以此作为回应，那么两者都不在上游。两种媒介都在回应同一种情感温度。

Shared beat sheet共享的节拍表

Before either produces final work on a chapter, the comic artist and composer share a beat sheet for that chapter's arc: the setup, the incident, the consequence, the closing beat. The music's structure (intro, build, drop, breakdown, outro) is then designed to mirror the chapter's narrative arc rather than mood-match it from the outside. The chapter and the track are the same event in two mediums simultaneously.

在各自为某章节产出最终作品之前，漫画作者与作曲人共享该章节弧线的节拍表：铺垫、事件、后果、收尾拍。音乐的结构（开场、推进、高潮、回落、尾声）随后被设计成镜像该章节的叙事弧线，而不是从外部进行情绪配合。章节与曲目，是同一个事件在两种媒介中同时发生的。

What's Yours to Shape什么是你要塑造的

The world isn't finished. The game isn't finished. You're in the room where both are still being decided, and what you bring as a comic artist shapes both of them, not just what they look like, but what they are.

这个世界还没有完成。游戏还没有完成。你置身于两者仍在被决定的房间里，而你作为漫画作者所带来的一切，塑造了这两者——不只是它们看起来的样子，而是它们究竟是什么。

The visual language of this IP is entirely open: what the creatures look like across their three stages, how the world beyond the arena is rendered, what the transformation arc feels like on a page. But those decisions don't live in a vacuum. How you answer them feeds back into the game's art direction, the world's emotional logic, what the other mediums reach for. The artist's instinct about how a creature holds its weight in base form is a contribution to the world, not just the comic.

这个 IP 的视觉语言完全是开放的：生物在三个阶段中各是什么样子，竞技场之外的世界如何被呈现，变化的弧线在页面上是什么感觉。但这些决策并不存在于真空之中。你如何回答它们，会反馈进游戏的美术方向、世界的情感逻辑，以及其他媒介所触及的东西。艺术家关于生物在基础形态下如何承载自身重量的本能判断，是对世界的贡献，而不只是对漫画的贡献。

The creatures don't exist yet in any committed form. The first time you and the composer and the game are in the room deciding what a creature looks and sounds like, that's a founding decision. It becomes the reference everything else is built from.

生物尚未以任何已落定的形式存在。第一次你与作曲人和游戏设计师同处一室，决定一个生物的外观与声音，那是一个奠基性的决策。它将成为所有其他一切据以建立的参照。

The comic's specific story, its characters, its point of entry into the world, are not decided. That's the creative work that belongs to you. The theme and the mechanic are load-bearing. The fiction that surrounds them is an invitation.

漫画具体的故事、它的角色、它切入这个世界的方式，都尚未决定。那是属于你的创作工作。主题与机制是承重的结构。围绕它们的虚构，是一份邀请。

You're building the visual language and the standalone comic that stands next to the game as an equal. And you're doing it while the world is still being made, which means you're making the world too.

你在建立视觉语言，建立那部与游戏并肩而立、地位平等的独立作品。而你是在这个世界仍在被创造的过程中做这一切的，这意味着你也在创造这个世界。
