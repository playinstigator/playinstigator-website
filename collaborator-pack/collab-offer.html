Ritual & Ruin · Collaborator Pack

Ritual & Ruin · 合作者资料包

A seat in something being built. 一个座位，在某件正在被建造的事情里。

We're building a transmedia IP world — a game and a set of co-created works across other mediums, launching this year. We're looking for one or two collaborators to join as co-creators: with rev share, full credit, and a real seat in shaping the world alongside the game.

我们正在建造一个跨媒介的 IP 世界——一款游戏，加上一组在其他媒介上共同创作的作品，今年发布。我们在寻找一两位合作者，以共同创作者的身份加入：有收益分成、完整署名，以及在游戏开发过程中真正参与塑造这个世界的座位。

What you'd actually make 你实际上会做的东西

Your medium isn't "the comic based on the game" or "the soundtrack for the game." It's an original expression of a world you helped design, built alongside the game — not after it. No primary medium. No hierarchy of canon. The game ships by end of 2026. Your medium's first release ships alongside or shortly after.

Below are three seats we're actively planning to fill. If your medium isn't listed but the model resonates, the conversation is still worth having.

你的媒介不是"基于游戏的漫画"或"为游戏配的原声"。它是对一个你参与设计的世界的原创表达，与游戏并行建造——而不是在游戏完成后才开始。没有主要媒介，没有正典层级。游戏在 2026 年底发布，你的媒介的第一次发布与之同步或紧随其后。

以下是我们正在积极寻找填补的三个座位。如果你的媒介不在列表中，但这套模型让你共鸣，对话依然值得展开。

Seat · Composer座位 · 作曲人

Audio & Music音频与音乐

You own the entire sonic world of the game — music, SFX, ambience — and write the standalone artefact that defines the IP outside it: at minimum a single defining song, or a small album if the work calls for it. The two halves feed each other. Sonic decisions in the game shape the standalone; the song's emotional core feeds back into how the game sounds.

你持有这款游戏整个声音世界——音乐、音效、环境音——同时写出在游戏之外定义这个 IP 的独立作品：最少是一首定义性单曲，如有需要也可以是小型专辑。两者相互喂养——游戏中的音色决定塑形独立作品，歌曲的情感核心反过来影响游戏的声音面貌。

Seat · Comic Artist座位 · 漫画创作者

Comic & Narrative Art漫画与叙事艺术

You build a parallel-medium artefact that proves the transmedia model in practice, not just on paper. Same world as the game, but a different entry point: the lore, the characters, the moments the game can't show. The creature before it completes a ritual. The cult that built the altars. The lab that watches and doesn't intervene. The comic can be found first — by readers who have never heard of the game — and pull them into the IP from an entirely different angle.

Visual canon decisions you make here ripple outward. How a creature holds its weight, what angle it looks most unsettling from, how decay changes its silhouette — that becomes the shared visual language everything else references.

你建造一件平行媒介作品，用实际行动（而不只是纸面上）证明这套跨媒介模型。与游戏同一个世界，但是不同的入口：游戏无法展示的传说、角色、时刻。仪式完成之前的那只生物。建造祭坛的教派。在旁观察却不介入的实验室。漫画可以被人先发现——被从未听说过这款游戏的读者发现——然后从一个完全不同的角度把他们拉进 IP。

你在这里做出的视觉正典决策会向外扩散：一只生物如何承担自身重量，从哪个角度看它最令人不安，腐化如何改变它的轮廓——这些都会成为其他一切所参照的共同视觉语言。

Seat · Toy & Product Designer座位 · 玩具与产品设计师

Physical Objects & Merch实体物品与周边

You design high-margin physical product lines — plushies, prints, apparel, enamel pins — that let an audience carry the IP in their hands. The form that makes creatures real outside a screen. The merch that turns fans into walking signals. This isn't an afterthought drop: the physical objects are designed as IP artefacts, with the same intentionality as the game or the comic. A plushie of a creature in its base form and another in its corrupted terminal form are two different emotional objects — and they're designed that way from the start.

You also bring a 3D spatial constraint that challenges everything else: building a creature to be manufactured and held in a hand reveals things the flat image got wrong, and the canon updates.

你设计高利润率的实体产品线——毛绒玩具、印刷品、服装、珐琅别针——让观众可以用双手持有这个 IP。让生物在屏幕之外变得真实的形式。把粉丝变成行走广告牌的周边。这不是事后草草上架的东西：实体物品作为 IP 成品来设计，与游戏或漫画具有同等的用心程度。一只生物在基础形态的毛绒玩具，和一只在腐化的终末形态的毛绒玩具，是两种不同的情感物件——并且从一开始就是这样被设计的。

你还带来了一个三维空间层面的制约，它会挑战其他一切：把一只生物做成可被制造、可被双手持握的东西，会揭示平面图像中那些不准确的地方，然后正典随之更新。

How these mediums feed each other 这些媒介如何互相喂养

The seats aren't parallel silos that happen to share a setting. The collective works because the mediums are in active conversation — decisions in one inform and sometimes reshape the others. Here's what that actually looks like in practice.

这些座位不是碰巧共享同一背景设定的平行孤岛。共创团体之所以能运作，是因为各媒介处于积极的对话之中——其中一个媒介的决策会影响、有时甚至重塑其他媒介。以下是这在实际操作中的样子。

Composer × Comic Artist作曲人 × 漫画创作者

The chapter that plays会"播放"的那一章

A chapter drops alongside a music release. The comic leads; the score follows — the final pages of a chapter establish a mood, and the track released the same day holds that mood as the reader sits with it. Or reversed: the music releases first, without explanation, and the chapter that arrives a week later visualises what the track was describing. Both pieces are complete without the other. Together they create a third thing that neither could carry alone.

Deeper: the composer's sense of how silence functions in the game — where the audio pulls back to let tension accumulate — is exactly what the artist is making visual decisions about in panel pacing. A conversation about one discipline changes the other.

一章漫画与一次音乐发布同步落地。漫画领先，配乐跟随——某章的最后几页建立了一种情绪，同日发布的曲目在读者停留其中时持续托住那种情绪。或者反过来：音乐先发布，没有任何说明，一周后到来的章节将那首曲子所描述的东西视觉化。两件作品各自都是完整的。合在一起，它们创造出一个两者单独都无法承载的第三样东西。

更深层：作曲人对游戏中"静默如何运作"的理解——音频在哪里收缩，以让紧张感积累——正是漫画创作者在分镜节奏上做视觉决策时所处理的事情。关于一门学科的对话，会改变另一门学科。

Comic Artist × Toy Designer漫画创作者 × 玩具设计师

What the creature actually is那只生物究竟是什么

The comic establishes the creature's visual canon — the silhouette, the weight, the way decay warps its form across the match's three stages. The toy is designed to that canon. But a physical object that has to be held in a hand, manufactured in volume, balanced on a shelf, exposes things that read fine on a flat page but don't resolve in three dimensions. The designer brings back constraints that reshape the artist's visual language — not as corrections, as refinement. The canonical creature ends up more considered than either discipline could have made it alone.

漫画建立生物的视觉正典——轮廓、重量、腐化如何在比赛三个阶段中扭曲它的形态。玩具依据这套正典来设计。但一个必须被双手持握、大批量生产、能在架子上保持平衡的实体物件，会暴露出平面页面上读起来没问题、但在三维空间中无法成立的东西。设计师带回了一些约束，这些约束重塑了漫画创作者的视觉语言——不是作为纠错，而是作为精炼。最终的正典生物，比任何一门学科单独所能做出的都更加严谨。

Composer × Toy Designer作曲人 × 玩具设计师

The reveal那场揭示

A new creature is introduced to the world. The toy shows the form; the score reveals the feeling. Neither can carry both at once: a plushie without sound is just shape, a track without an image is just tone. Together, dropping the same week, they establish what the creature is — its emotional register — in a way that a screenshot or a trailer can't. This is also a distribution experiment: the toy finds an audience that doesn't follow game accounts; the music finds listeners who won't see the Steam page. Both pull toward the same IP from different sides.

一只新生物被介绍给世界。玩具展示形态，配乐揭示感觉。两者都无法同时承载另一方的内容：一只没有声音的毛绒玩具只是形状，一首没有图像的曲子只是音调。合在一起，同一周落地，它们以截图或预告片所无法做到的方式建立了这只生物是什么——它的情感音域。这同时也是一个分发实验：玩具触达不关注游戏账号的受众；音乐触达不会看 Steam 页面的听众。两者从不同方向拉向同一个 IP。

All three三者同时

The same moment from different angles从不同角度抵达同一个时刻

The terminal phase — a creature completing two rituals, transforming into something that shouldn't exist, beginning to hunt. A chapter ends on that transformation. The music that plays when you open the page. The plushie on your shelf already in the corrupted form. Three mediums landing the same emotional beat simultaneously, from different directions, for audiences who may have entered the IP through any one of them. That's the Ghibli coherence model applied in practice: not a style guide, but a shared emotional language built by people who've been in conversation long enough to trust each other's judgment.

终末阶段——一只生物完成了两次仪式，转化成某种不该存在的东西，开始猎杀。一章漫画在那次变形处结束。打开页面时响起的音乐。架子上的毛绒玩具已经是腐化的形态。三种媒介同时从不同方向抵达同一个情感节拍，面向可能通过其中任何一种进入这个 IP 的受众。这就是 Ghibli 一致性模型的实践形态：不是风格指南，而是由经过足够长时间对话、已经能相互信任判断力的人们共同建立起来的情感语言。

What this will actually require of you 这件事实际上会向你要求什么

We need to be honest about what saying yes means in practice. Not to scare anyone off — but because the wrong fit hurts both sides.

我们需要诚实地告诉你：说"是"在实际操作中意味着什么。不是要吓跑任何人——而是因为错配对双方都是伤害。

Your evenings and weekends你的晚上和周末

Until the IP is producing stable revenue, this is a side commitment for everyone — including the founder. What we need is that your weekends are mostly available for this work: ideation sessions, deeper creative conversations, the integrated work between mediums. Evenings and late nights are for shorter check-ins and async. If your weekends are fully booked and immovable, this won't work.

在 IP 产生稳定收入之前，这件事对每个人来说都是"额外的承诺"——包括创始人在内。我们需要的是你的周末大部分时间可以为这件事腾出来：构思讨论、更深入的创作对话、跨媒介之间需要整合的工作。晚上和深夜用来做较短的 check-in 和异步工作。如果你的周末都已被排满且挪不动，那这件事就行不通。

Tolerance for creative friction对创作摩擦的容忍

Doing co-design across mediums is harder than doing your own thing. You will disagree with people. People will disagree with you. There will be stretches where nothing is converging. That's not the model failing — that's what creative rigour at this scale actually looks like. Anyone who needs every conversation to end in alignment will burn out fast.

在多个媒介之间共同设计，比单独做自己的东西难得多。你会和别人意见相反，别人也会和你意见相反。会有一段时间感觉一切都没有在收拢。这不是模型在失败——这就是这种规模上的创作严谨性真实的样子。任何需要"每次对话都以达成一致告终"的人，会很快把自己烧光。

Self-driven craft growth自我驱动的手艺成长

Within your own medium, you are the only expert. No one else on the team will mentor you in your craft. We can challenge you about how your work meets the world, the story, the other mediums. We can't teach you the craft itself. That responsibility is entirely yours.

在你自己那门媒介之内，你是唯一的那个专家。团队里没有任何其他人能在你的手艺上指导你。我们可以就"你的工作如何与这个世界、这个故事、其他媒介相遇"来挑战你。我们没办法教你这门手艺本身。那份责任完全由你承担。

Extraordinary work is extraordinarily difficult. That's not a warning — it's the whole point. If this were easy, someone would already have done it. 非凡的工作就是非凡地难。这不是一句警告——这就是事情的全部要点。如果这事很简单，早就有人做过了。

01 · The IP Model01 · IP 模型

IP is ExperienceIP 即体验

IP is not a set of owned assets. It is the total experience an audience has across every medium, every encounter, every conversation. Why work made with a game (not for it) becomes part of how the IP exists in the world.

IP 不是一组被持有的资产。IP 是观众在每一个媒介、每一次相遇、每一段对话中所获得的总体体验。为什么"与游戏一起做出来的作品"（而不是"为了游戏做的作品"）会成为 IP 在世界上存在方式的一部分。
