The financial and ownership structure for Play Instigator's transmedia IP collective. A working reference, not a summary — every structural decision is explained alongside the reasoning that produced it.

This is what we've worked out as our best attempt to make the financials as fair as possible for everyone — but the numbers are open for renegotiation with the primary cohort of collaborators. Foundational Principle: Craft as Capital基础原则：创作即资本

The entire structure rests on one foundational reframe: contributors are not employees waiting to be paid. They are co-investors whose currency is craft rather than cash.

整个结构建立在一次基础性的重新框定之上：贡献者不是等着被支付的雇员。他们是共同投资人，只是他们的货币是"创作"而不是"现金"。

In the early stage of any creative project, there is no money to fund production. A game costs significant capital to make. So does an animation. So does a manga volume. The conventional answers — find an investor, take a publisher advance, secure a grant large enough to cover everything — either dilute the collective to non-creative stakeholders, mortgage future revenue, or simply don't scale to what's realistically available to a bootstrapped collective.

在任何创作项目的早期，都没有钱可以拿来给制作做资金支持。一款游戏要花相当多的资本去做。一部动画也是。一卷漫画也是。常规的解决方案——找投资人、拿出版商预付款、拿到一笔大到能覆盖一切的资助——要么把这个团体稀释给非创作型的利益相关方、要么把未来收益抵押出去，要么干脆和"自筹资金的团体在现实中能拿到的资源量"对不上。

This collective takes the honest path: each Type 1 medium manager funds their own production. Their software, hardware, outsourced work, time — all of it is their capital contribution alongside their creative contribution. In return, they get a structurally large share of their medium's direct revenue during a recoup period, designed to recover that investment first and generate return after.

这个共创团体走诚实的那条路：每一位 Type 1 媒介负责人为自己的制作出资。他们的软件、硬件、外包工作、时间——所有这些都是他们的资本贡献，与他们的创作贡献并行。作为回报，他们在一个"回收期"里能拿到自己媒介直接收入中结构性较大的一份，被设计成"先把投资拿回来，再产生回报"。

There's an important consequence here that's worth naming explicitly: each medium manager is accountable to their own expenses. Nobody is taking the collective's money and spending it without producing — at this stage, there is no pool of collective funds to draw from in the first place. The money you spend on your medium's production is your money, your decision, and your risk. The recoup share you receive later is calibrated to make you whole on that investment before any of it is shared with the rest of the collective.

这里有一件值得明确说出来的重要推论：每一位媒介负责人都对自己的开支负责。没有人能拿"团体的钱"去花掉却不交付任何结果——在现阶段，根本就不存在一个可以让人支取的"团体资金池"。你花在自己媒介制作上的钱，是你自己的钱、你自己的决定、你自己的风险。你之后拿到的回收份额，是被精心校准的——目的是让你这笔投入在与团体其他人分享任何收益之前，先被全数收回。

This reframe makes the collective viable without external investors. It keeps individual accountability intact — your reward from your medium is a direct function of how well you executed it, not an averaged outcome diluted by how the collective performed in aggregate. And it provides the honest pitch to collaborators: you are co-investing, the risk is real and shared, the structure exists to fairly distribute returns when they arrive.

这种重新框定，让这个共创团体在没有外部投资人的情况下也能成立。它让个人问责保持完整——你从你的媒介里拿到的回报，直接是你执行得有多好的函数，而不是被"整个团体的总体表现"平均稀释后的结果。它也给到合作者一个诚实的话术：你是在共同投资，风险是真实的、是共担的，这个结构存在的目的，是为了在收益到来时公平地分配它。

Medium Taxonomy媒介分类

Mediums fall into three categories. The categories are economic, not creative — they describe how each medium relates to revenue, not how creatively significant it is.

媒介分为三大类。这些类别是从经济角度划分的，不是从创作角度——它们描述的是每种媒介如何与"收入"发生关系，而不是它在创作上多重要。

Type 1 — Product Mediums (Direct Revenue)Type 1 — 产品媒介（直接收入）

Mediums that generate direct, attributable revenue. A composer earns streaming royalties; a game designer earns sales revenue; a manga creator earns volume sales. Each Type 1 medium operates on the self-funded production model — manager bears costs, recoups through direct revenue, then shares ongoing revenue with the collective.

能够产生直接、可归属收入的媒介。作曲人赚流媒体版税；游戏设计师赚销售收入；漫画作者赚单行本销售。每一种 Type 1 媒介都按"自筹资金的制作模式"运作——负责人承担成本，通过直接收入回收，然后再与整个团体分享后续收入。

Type 1 mediums are further classified along one axis: how much of the revenue is generated by craft versus by IP equity built by others.

Type 1 媒介还会沿着一条轴线再细分：这部分收入有多少是由"创作本身"产出的，又有多少是由"其他人共同建造起来的 IP 资产"产出的。

Craft-Driven创作驱动

The medium itself is the product. Audiences pay for the experience the craft delivers. A great game stands alone; weak IP doesn't tank a great game. Examples: games, novels, films, comics, soundtracks, original animation. The IP is a tailwind, not the engine.

媒介本身就是产品。观众为创作所交付的体验付费。一款优秀的游戏可以独立成立；弱 IP 不会拖垮一款优秀的游戏。例子：游戏、小说、电影、漫画、原声带、原创动画。 IP 是顺风，不是引擎。

IP-LeveragedIP 借力

The medium primarily monetises IP equity built across the collective. Audiences buy because they recognise and love the IP. The craft of execution still matters — bad design kills a product line — but the IP is doing most of the selling. Examples: merchandise, plushies, posters, themed events, branded collaborations.

这类媒介主要把整个团体共同建造的 IP 资产变现。观众购买，是因为他们认得并喜爱这个 IP。执行的工艺依然重要——糟糕的设计会让一条产品线垮掉——但卖东西的主要还是 IP。例子：周边、毛绒玩具、海报、主题活动、品牌联名。

Type 2 — Structural Audience Mediums (No Direct Revenue)Type 2 — 结构性受众媒介（无直接收入）

ARG, community design, social media storytelling, cosplay-as-medium, transmedia experiments. These build genuine IP value and contribute canon, but cannot monetise directly by their nature. Their value flows entirely indirectly — through audience growth and engagement that converts elsewhere across the IP.

ARG（替代现实游戏）、社群设计、社交媒体叙事、把 cosplay 当作媒介、各种跨媒介实验。这些媒介确实在建造真实的 IP 价值并贡献正典，但按其本性，无法直接变现。它们的价值完全是间接流转的——通过受众增长和参与度，最终在 IP 的其他地方完成转化。

Type 2 mediums break the self-funded model: there is no direct revenue to recoup against. They require either grant funding or collective subsidy to exist, and they are compensated through unit-based collective revenue distributions and earned contribution recognition rather than direct medium revenue.

Type 2 媒介打破了自筹资金的模式：没有直接收入可以拿来回收成本。它们需要靠资助或团体补贴才能存在，且其报酬来自基于"单位"的共同收入分配，以及"贡献份额"的认可，而不是来自单一媒介的直接收入。

Experience Mediums (Creative Marketing as Type 2)体验型媒介（把创意营销当作 Type 2）

How the audience discovers, engages with, and participates in the IP is itself a designed creative space. A behind-the-scenes documentary of a cross-medium creative session is simultaneously original IP content, audience building, and a demonstration of the collective's philosophy.

观众如何发现 IP、如何与之互动、如何参与其中，这本身就是一块需要被设计的创作空间。一部记录跨媒介创作会议的幕后纪录片，同时是 IP 原创内容、是受众建设，也是对这个共创团体哲学的一次演示。

In this collective, creative marketing is not a cost centre — it is an Experience Medium, treated structurally as Type 2. The line between Experience Medium and operational marketing is whether the output adds to the IP itself (Experience Medium) or merely points at the IP (operational).

在这个共创团体里，创意营销不是成本中心——它是"体验型媒介"，在结构上按 Type 2 处理。"体验型媒介"和"运营性营销"之间的分界，在于产出究竟是"为 IP 本身添加东西"（体验型媒介），还是仅仅"指向 IP"（运营性）。

The Direct Medium Split直接媒介收入的分配

Direct medium revenue from a shipped Type 1 work, after the operating fund cut, is distributed through a two-phase model. Every medium with production capital passes through both phases sequentially. Mediums with zero production capital skip Phase 1.

来自一个已发布的 Type 1 作品的直接媒介收入，在扣除运营基金后，按两阶段模型进行分配。任何投入了制作资本的媒介都依次经过两个阶段。完全没有投入制作资本的媒介跳过第 1 阶段。

Phase 1 — Recoup (Universal: 80% Manager / 20% Earned Contribution)第 1 阶段 — 回收（通用：负责人 80% / 贡献份额 20%）

When a Type 1 medium ships with production capital at risk, the manager has typically borne significant costs out of their own pocket. They need to recover that capital before any steady-state split would be fair.

当一个 Type 1 媒介带着制作资本风险被发布时，负责人通常已经从自己口袋里承担了相当可观的成本。在任何"稳态分配"对他们而言才公平之前，他们需要先把那些资本拿回来。

While the medium's recoup balance is non-zero: 80% to medium manager, 20% to earned contribution. IP equity routing (relevant only for IP-Leveraged Phase 2) is paused — the manager bears 100% of the capital risk and is paid back first.

在该媒介的回收余额还没有清零之前：负责人 80%，贡献份额 20%。 IP 资产分流（只在"IP 借力"媒介的第 2 阶段才相关）被暂停——负责人承担了 100% 的资本风险，被优先偿付。

Once cumulative direct revenue from that medium has fully reimbursed the manager's tracked production costs, the medium exits Phase 1 and enters Phase 2 at its tier's split.

当来自该媒介的累计直接收入完全偿付了负责人被记录在案的制作成本之后，该媒介就退出第 1 阶段，按它所属层级对应的比例进入第 2 阶段。

What counts toward recoup哪些算入回收

  • Direct production costs: software licences, hardware purchased for that medium, outsourced work, assets and materials
  • 直接制作成本：软件授权、为该媒介购置的硬件、外包工作、素材与材料
  • For IP-Leveraged: manufacturing, MOQs, tooling, prototypes, initial inventory infrastructure, substantial upfront platform fees
  • 对于"IP 借力"媒介：生产制造、最低起订量（MOQ）、模具、样品、初始库存与发货基础设施、较大额的前期平台费用
  • Not labour: the manager's own time is already compensated through the revenue split itself
  • 不算劳动：负责人自己的时间已经通过收入分配本身得到补偿了
  • Not ongoing operational costs: per-unit shipping, recurring storage, day-to-day customer service
  • 不算持续性运营成本：单件发货成本、日常仓储、日常客户服务

Why 80/20 not 100/0为什么是 80/20 而不是 100/0 A pure 100/0 model would sever the cross-medium incentive during the riskiest phase of the project — exactly when collaborators most need to feel they are participating in each other's success. The 80/20 split keeps the earned contribution share material, signals that cross-medium contribution is acknowledged immediately, and still routes the bulk of early revenue to the person who fronted the capital. 纯粹的 100/0 模型，会在项目风险最高的那个阶段把跨媒介激励切断——而那恰恰正是合作者最需要感觉自己"参与在彼此的成功里"的时候。80/20 这套分配让"贡献份额"那一部分依然是有分量的、立刻就传达出"跨媒介贡献是被认可的"这个信号，同时仍把早期收入的绝大部分送到那个垫付资本的人手里。

The IP-Leveraged manager share is structurally lower not as punishment but as honest economics: a plushie maker is competent and indispensable, but they are selling a character that others helped build. The 25% direct return plus unit-based participation in collective revenue still gives them meaningful upside; it just doesn't pretend they generated all the value.

Earned Contribution Share贡献份额

The earned contribution share is decided collectively at the end of production, before the work ships, before revenue is known. It rewards cross-medium integration: who from outside the medium meaningfully shaped the work — whose musical idea changed a mechanic, whose visual concept reshaped the narrative structure.

"贡献份额"是在制作末期由整个团体共同决定的——在作品发布之前，在收入还未知的时候。它奖励的是跨媒介整合：来自该媒介之外的哪些人真的塑形了这部作品——是谁的音乐想法改变了一个机制，是谁的视觉概念重塑了叙事结构。

Medium managers themselves are eligible for earned contribution share in other mediums they cross-contributed to. They are not eligible for earned contribution in their own medium; that work is already compensated through the manager share. Earning contribution by working with other medium managers is the explicit cross-medium incentive built into the model.

媒介负责人自己有资格在他们跨界贡献过的其他媒介中拿到贡献份额。他们没有资格在自己的媒介里拿贡献份额；那部分工作已经由"负责人份额"补偿了。通过与其他媒介负责人合作而赚到贡献份额，正是这个模型里明确内建的跨媒介激励。

The Three Tests for adjudicating contribution判定贡献的"三道测试"

  1. Specificity.具体性。 Did the contribution contain creative substance specific to this work, or was it generic input that could have applied to anything? Generic ("you should add more variety") fails. Specific ("the ritual scene needs a 7/8 time signature to mirror the broken rhythm of the chant") passes. 这次贡献里有没有针对这部作品的具体创作实质，还是只是任何作品都能套用的泛泛建议？泛泛的（"你应该加多一点变化"）通不过。具体的（"那场仪式戏需要 7/8 拍的节拍，来对应那段吟唱断裂的节奏"）通过。
  2. Causality.因果性。 Would the integration have ended up in the work without this specific contribution? If the team would have arrived there independently, contribution earns nothing. If unclear, share is reduced. If the integration directly traces to this contribution, continue. 没有这次具体的贡献，这种整合是否依然会出现在作品里？如果团队本来就会独立地走到那里，这次贡献得不到任何份额。如果不清楚，份额被削减。如果整合可以直接追溯到这次贡献，继续下一道测试。
  3. Substance.实质性。 How much of the actual creative content of the integration came from this contribution versus the medium manager's execution? Low (surfaced a connection) → small share. High (contained the creative concept) → large share. 这种整合里"真正的创作内容"，有多少是由这次贡献提供的，又有多少是由媒介负责人执行出来的？低（只是浮现了一种关联）→ 小份额。高（贡献本身就包含了那个创作概念）→ 大份额。

Hard prerequisite: the contribution must be in the Cross-Medium Collaboration Log maintained by the facilitator. Anyone who wants their cross-medium contribution to count must ensure it is captured at the time of the conversation.

硬性前提：这次贡献必须出现在召集人维护的"跨媒介协作日志"里。任何希望自己的跨媒介贡献被算入的人，都必须确保它在那场对话发生的当时就被记录下来。

The Orphan Pool"无主池"

When a medium ships in true isolation — zero cross-medium contributions worth recognising — the earned contribution share has no claimants. That share routes to the collective revenue stream rather than reverting to the medium manager.

当一个媒介真的是孤立地完成发布——没有任何值得被承认的跨媒介贡献——那"贡献份额"就没有任何人可以认领。这一份会流入共同收入池，而不是回到媒介负责人手上。

The cost of isolation is therefore not a reduction in this work's manager share — it is the absence of reciprocal earned contribution stakes from collaborators the manager didn't bring in. A manager who actively integrates accumulates earned contribution shares across other mediums over time. The system rewards collaboration through reciprocity, not through penalising solo work on any single deliverable.

因此，"孤立工作"的代价不是这部作品里负责人份额被削减——而是从那些"负责人没有拉进来"的合作者那一侧，本来可能拿到的"互相对应的贡献份额"没有了。一位主动整合的负责人，会随着时间在其他媒介里累积起一份份贡献份额。整个系统通过"互惠"来奖励协作，而不是通过"惩罚任何单一交付物上的独自工作"来奖励协作。

Rolling 12-Month Units12 个月滚动单位

Collective revenue is distributed through a single rolling 12-month unit mechanism. Each contributor accumulates units monthly based on their roles, and only the last 12 months of units count toward any distribution.

共同收入通过一套统一的"12 个月滚动单位"机制来分配。每一位贡献者根据自己所担任的角色按月累积单位，每一次分配只计入最近 12 个月的单位。

一位贡献者每月的累积 = 召集人角色（若担任）+ 他所持有的所有媒介按层级权重的总和。

Activity tiers, defined活动层级的定义

Active. The medium is producing meaningful content for the IP this period — releases, sessions, real output. Low. Some activity, but minimal — passive engagement, occasional output, the medium is breathing but not driving. Dormant. No activity this period. There is no separate "contributor status" axis on top of this — everything is captured by what each medium and role is actually doing in a given month.

活跃。该媒介在当月为 IP 产出了有意义的内容——发布、会议、真实的产出。低活动。有一些活动，但很少——被动的受众触达、偶尔的产出，这个媒介还在呼吸，但没有在推进。休眠。当月完全没有活动。在这之上没有任何独立的"贡献者状态"维度——一切都由每个媒介和每个角色当月实际做了什么来体现。

How tiers get assigned — and challenged层级如何被赋予——以及如何被质询

Each contributor self-assigns the tier of their mediums and roles each month, based on what was actually happening. Honest self-reporting is the default. But the collective has two checks.

每位贡献者每月根据实际发生的事情，自行给自己持有的媒介和角色赋予层级。诚实的自我申报是默认。但共创团体保留两道核查机制。

Active claims that aren't backed by real work. If a medium is claimed as Active but produced very little real activity that month, any member can call a vote to demote it to Low for that period. Simple majority demotes. The vote isn't punitive — it's a tier correction for that single month, and the contributor can claim Active again next month if real activity returns. This works because in this collective, what each medium ships and shares each month is visible to everyone.

"申报为活跃，但没有真实工作支撑"的情况。如果某个媒介被申报为"活跃"，但当月实际产出极少，任何成员都可以发起表决，将该媒介在那一个月降至"低活动"。简单多数即降级通过。这个表决不是惩罚性的——它只是某个月里对层级的修正，下个月只要实际活动恢复，仍可重新申报为"活跃"。它之所以行得通，是因为在这个共创团体里，每个媒介每个月所发布、所分享的东西，对所有人来说都是可见的。

Mediums stuck in Dormant. If a medium remains Dormant for six or more months without renewed activity, any member can call a vote to reassign that medium to a new manager or formally end it. The original manager's vested earnings on past shipped works are unaffected — those persist regardless.

长期卡在"休眠"的媒介。如果某个媒介连续六个月以上保持"休眠"且没有恢复活动，任何成员都可以发起表决，把该媒介重新分配给新的负责人，或正式结束这个媒介。原负责人在过往已发布作品上的既得收益不受影响——这些都会继续保留。

The intent of both checks is the same: nobody quietly accrues full units while their medium is in fact dormant, and no medium is held permanently by an absent manager. The collective's own visibility and willingness to name it keeps the model honest without needing a formal audit process.

这两道核查的意图是一样的：没有人能在媒介事实上已休眠的情况下悄悄按全速累积单位，也没有任何媒介会被一个长期缺席的负责人无限期占着不放。团体自身的可见性、加上"愿意把这件事说出来"的意愿，无需任何正式审计流程，就能让这套模型保持诚实。

Worked example具体示例

  • Alice holds Medium A (game, Active) and Medium B (soundtrack, Low) → 1.5 units/month
  • Alice 持有媒介 A（游戏，活跃）和媒介 B（原声带，低活动）→ 每月 1.5 单位
  • Bob holds Medium C (animation, Active) → 1.0 unit/month
  • Bob 持有媒介 C（动画，活跃）→ 每月 1.0 单位
  • Carol holds Medium D (community, Active) → 1.0 unit/month
  • Carol 持有媒介 D（社群，活跃）→ 每月 1.0 单位
  • Dan is the facilitator (no medium held) → 1.0 unit/month
  • Dan 是召集人（不持有任何媒介）→ 每月 1.0 单位

After 12 months: Alice 18, Bob 12, Carol 12, Dan 12 — total 54 units.

12 个月之后：Alice 18，Bob 12，Carol 12，Dan 12 — 总计 54 单位。

Collective revenue this month = $7,000. Per-unit value = $7,000 ÷ 54 = $129.63.

本月共同收入 = $7,000。每单位价值 = $7,000 ÷ 54 = $129.63。

  • Alice: 18 × $129.63 = $2,333
  • Bob: 12 × $129.63 = $1,556
  • Carol: 12 × $129.63 = $1,556
  • Dan: 12 × $129.63 = $1,556

Why smoothed over 12 months为什么要按 12 个月平滑 Collective revenue this month — merchandise sales, licensing payments, IP equity routing — is generated by IP value built up over months and years, not by what someone happened to do this month. Distributing entirely by current-month tier would be structurally unfair to a contributor who was active for 11 months and went dormant this month. The same logic applies in reverse: a contributor who joined 3 months ago accumulates units gradually as their window fills, reaching full-rate participation at the 12-month mark. This protects existing contributors from immediate dilution when new joiners come on. 本月的共同收入——周边销售、授权款项、IP 资产分流——是由数月乃至数年积累起来的 IP 价值产生的，而不是由"本月某个人恰好做了什么"产生的。完全按"本月层级"来分配，对一位整整活跃了 11 个月、本月才进入休眠的贡献者而言，结构上是不公平的。反过来同样成立：一位 3 个月前才加入的贡献者，会随着自己的滚动窗口被填满而逐步累积单位，到第 12 个月才达到全速参与。这保护了既有贡献者，让他们不会在新成员加入时立刻被稀释。

The income-ceiling asymmetry收入天花板的不对称

Type 1 medium managers earn through both direct revenue (with recoup) and unit-based collective distributions. Type 2 medium managers earn through unit-based distributions and earned contribution only. A successful game generates direct revenue that animation simply cannot match by structure.

Type 1 媒介负责人通过两条路径赚钱：直接收入（带回收期）和基于单位的共同分配。Type 2 媒介负责人只通过基于单位的分配以及"贡献份额"来赚钱。一款成功的游戏所产生的直接收入，从结构上来说，动画就是赶不上。

This is the honest economic reality of different mediums, not a flaw to engineer around. The honest pitch to a Type 2 contributor is: your medium is critical to the IP's success but won't generate direct revenue. Your share of the IP's collective value is real and grows as the IP grows. You are taking more income-volatility risk than a Type 1 manager in the short term.

这是不同媒介之间诚实的经济现实，不是一个需要被工程化绕过去的"缺陷"。对一位 Type 2 贡献者诚实的话术是：你这门媒介对 IP 的成功是关键的，但它不会产生直接收入。你在 IP 共同价值里那一份是真实的，会随着 IP 的成长而成长。短期内，你比 Type 1 负责人承担更大的"收入波动"风险。

Type 2 pre-revenue funding bridgeType 2 在收入到来前的过渡资金机制

A real structural gap exists in early stage: Type 2 medium managers build IP equity now but earn from it later. Three bridge mechanisms address this:

早期阶段存在一个真实的结构性缺口：Type 2 媒介负责人是现在就在搭建 IP 资产，却要在晚一些才能从中赚钱。三套过渡机制来应对这一点：

  1. Grant funding for Type 2 production directly.直接用资助来支持 Type 2 制作。 The cleanest bridge — doesn't dilute equity, doesn't burden the operating fund long-term, aligns with what grant bodies want to fund. 最干净的过渡方式——不会稀释股权、不会长期拖累运营基金、又恰好对应资助机构愿意资助的对象。
  2. Operating fund stipend as recoup-able advance.把运营基金里的小额津贴，当作"可回收的预付款"。 A small monthly stipend during the pre-revenue period, treated as a recoup-able advance against future unit distributions. Conditional on the operating fund having capacity. 在收入还未到来的那段时间里，每月发一笔小额津贴，被视作"对未来基于单位的分配的可回收预付款"。前提是运营基金本身有这个余力。
  3. Cross-medium earned contribution from Type 1 mediums.从 Type 1 媒介那一侧赚到的跨媒介贡献份额。 A Type 2 animator who shapes a Type 1 game's visual language earns from the game's earned contribution share — real income from Type 1 revenue streams that does not wait for collective revenue to materialise. 一位 Type 2 的动画师如果塑形了某款 Type 1 游戏的视觉语言，就可以从那款游戏的"贡献份额"里拿到收入——这是来自 Type 1 收入流的真实收入，不需要等到共同收入真正出现才能拿到。

Departure离开

Departure is the formal event of leaving the collective — handing over your mediums, releasing any roles you held, and no longer being part of the collective's governance. When it happens:

"离开"指的是正式退出共创团体的那个具体事件——把你持有的媒介交出去、放下你所担任的角色、不再参与团体的治理。它发生时：

What persists permanently: medium manager share from works you shipped while you were holding the medium; ongoing recoup payments on those works until the balance hits zero; earned contribution shares already locked at ship time. None of these depend on remaining in the collective.

永久保留的部分：你持有那个媒介期间所发布作品的媒介负责人份额；那些作品上仍在进行的回收支付，直到余额清零为止；在发布时已被锁定的贡献份额。这些都不取决于你是否继续留在共创团体里。

What stops: unit accrual, because you no longer hold any mediums or roles. Existing units in your rolling 12-month window phase out naturally over the following twelve months.

停止的部分：单位累积停止，因为你已不再持有任何媒介或角色。你滚动 12 个月窗口里既有的单位会在接下来的十二个月里自然滚出。

What stays with the IP: all creative contributions to the IP world (characters, world elements, mechanics, lore) remain part of the IP regardless of who built them. This is foundational and must be agreed upfront.

留在 IP 里的部分：对 IP 世界的所有创作性贡献（角色、世界元素、机制、设定史）都留在 IP 里，与是谁创造的无关。这是地基性的条款，必须在前期就达成共识。

What happens to the mediums you were holding: reassigned to a new manager, archived, or formally ended — by collective agreement at the time of departure. (See also Mediums stuck in Dormant above for the case where someone fades away without a formal departure.)

你曾持有的媒介会怎样：由整个团体在你离开之时共同协商，转交给新的负责人、归档存放，或正式结束。（关于"有人没有正式离开但渐渐隐没"的情况，另见上文"长期卡在'休眠'的媒介"。）

Facilitator Compensation召集人的报酬

The facilitator has two distinct roles, with two separate compensation mechanisms.

召集人有两种不同的角色，对应两套分开的报酬机制。

Role 1 — Operational: Fixed Monthly Stipend角色 1 — 运营性：固定月津贴

Coordination, financial reporting, running cross-medium sessions, managing agreements, onboarding, dispute handling, grant chasing, ledger maintenance. Funded from the operating fund. A stipend rather than a percentage of revenue is deliberate — a percentage incentivises operational decisions that favour revenue generation over other collective values.

协调、财务报告、主持跨媒介会议、管理协议、新成员入驻、纠纷处理、申请资助、维护各类账本。由运营基金支付。采用"津贴"而不是"按收入分成"是有意为之——按比例分成会激励召集人在运营决策上偏向"产生更多收入"，而压过其他团体价值。

Benchmarked against local market rate (Singapore: SGD 4,000–7,000/month full-time; SGD 1,500–3,500/month part-time at early stage). At founding, the stipend is set at what the collective can afford AND what the facilitator can accept. The gap is the facilitator's own version of "craft as capital" — investing professional time at below-market rate in exchange for equity stake.

参照本地市场价（新加坡：全职 SGD 4,000–7,000/月；早期阶段兼职 SGD 1,500–3,500/月）。在创始阶段，津贴金额定在"团体能负担" 且"召集人能接受"的那个交集上。两者之间的差额，就是召集人版本的"创作即资本"——以低于市场价的方式投入专业时间，换取股权。

Role 2 — Creative/Vision: Earned Contribution and Units角色 2 — 创作 / 愿景：贡献份额与单位

The facilitator does not hold a medium, so they do not receive automatic medium manager shares. They are eligible for earned contribution shares through the same mechanism as anyone else — for any deliverable they meaningfully shaped through cross-medium contribution. They also accumulate units at the standard rate for the facilitator role.

召集人不持有任何一种媒介，所以他们不会自动拿到任何媒介负责人份额。他们通过和其他所有人完全一样的机制有资格拿到贡献份额——只要他们通过跨媒介贡献真正塑形了某一项交付物。他们也按"召集人角色"的标准速率累积单位。

The separation matters: the facilitator should not be receiving creative-stake compensation simply for having had the original idea or holding the title. Their creative stake grows because they actually shape work, deliverable by deliverable.

把这两件事分开很重要：召集人不应该仅仅因为"是最初提出想法的人"或者"挂着这个头衔"就得到创作层面的股权。他们的创作权益应该是随着他们一项一项地真正塑形作品而增长的。

The Cross-Medium Collaboration Log跨媒介协作日志

The earned contribution conversation depends on accurate memory of what cross-medium contributions actually shaped each deliverable. Memory months after the fact is unreliable. The facilitator maintains a Cross-Medium Collaboration Log alongside the recoup ledger.

"贡献份额"那场对话依赖于"哪些跨媒介贡献真的塑形了每一项交付物"的准确记忆。事后几个月的记忆是不可靠的。召集人在维护回收账本的同时，也维护一份"跨媒介协作日志"。

Every cross-medium conversation and discussion is recorded — audio or video — alongside the qualitative log entry. This serves two distinct purposes:

每一次跨媒介对话与讨论都会被录下——音频或视频——与文字描述的日志条目一起留档。这件事服务两个完全不同的目的：

Traceability可追溯

Recordings are the primary evidence layer for the retrospective earned contribution conversation. A logged claim is corroborated by the actual recording. This eliminates memory disputes and makes the Three Tests applicable to verifiable evidence rather than subjective recall.

录音录像是事后那场"贡献份额"对话的主要证据层。一条被写在日志里的主张，可以通过实际录像得到佐证。这消除了"记忆之争"，让"三道测试"得以应用在可被验证的证据上，而不是主观回忆上。

Source Material原素材

Recordings are the raw material for Experience Medium output: behind-the-scenes documentaries, devlog excerpts, clips of cross-medium creative sessions. Recording everything makes this Experience Medium concept genuinely operational rather than aspirational.

这些录像就是"体验型媒介"产出的原素材：幕后纪录片、开发日志的节选、跨媒介创作会议的剪辑片段。把一切都录下来，让"体验型媒介"这个概念真正变得可操作，而不是只停留在理想中。

Pre-Revenue Operating Fund Seeding收入到来前的运营基金启动资金

Before any revenue exists, the operating fund needs to be seeded. Marketing tools, infrastructure, legal setup, the first paid ad, basic admin: these need actual cash. Someone has to put money in.

在任何收入出现之前，运营基金需要被启动。营销工具、基础设施、法律事项的搭建、第一笔付费广告、基础行政事务：这些都需要真实的现金。总要有人把钱放进去。

Sources of seed capital启动资金的来源

  1. Founder cash contribution (open-ended and iterative).创始人的现金贡献（开放式、迭代式）。 The founder seeds the operating fund as expenses arise — paying for what is needed when it is needed and accumulating those payments in the operating fund capital ledger as receipts. There is no fixed upfront target dollar amount. The pre-flight check is "are you committed to ensuring the collective can function," which is the structural baseline of being a founder. 创始人是按"开支出现"为运营基金注资——需要的时候支付需要的金额，并把这些支付以收据的形式累积在"运营基金资本账本"里。没有固定的预先目标金额。"飞行前检查"问的是"你是否承诺让这个共创团体能够运作下去"——这是身为创始人的结构性底线。
  2. Optional collective member cash contributions.团体成员可选的现金贡献。 Members may choose to add cash alongside their craft contribution. Welcomed but not required. Members who don't contribute cash are full co-owners through their craft contribution alone. 成员可以选择在创作贡献之外，再加上现金贡献。我们欢迎，但不要求。不投入现金的成员，仅凭创作贡献就是完整意义上的共同所有者。
  3. Grants secured for operating costs.为运营成本争取到的资助。 Some grant bodies will fund operating costs of indie creative collectives, not just production. 某些资助机构会资助独立创作团体的运营成本，而不仅仅是制作成本。
  4. Crowdfunding tied to the first deliverable.绑定首个交付物的众筹。 A Kickstarter or Patreon for the first Type 1 deliverable typically funds production and the deliverable's own marketing. 围绕第一个 Type 1 交付物开展的 Kickstarter 或 Patreon，通常用来资助该交付物的制作以及它自己的营销。

How cash contributions are treated现金贡献如何处理

Cash contributions are tracked recoup-able balances, not equity dilution. A contributor's stake is craft-based — none of these change because they contributed cash. The cash contribution is a loan to the collective, repayable from operating fund surplus.

现金贡献被记录为"可回收余额"，而不是"股权稀释"。一位贡献者的股权是基于创作的——这一切都不会因为他出了现金而改变。现金贡献是对共创团体的借款，由运营基金盈余偿还。

Repaid pro-rata from operating fund surplus, before any distributions to anyone. Time spent on collective work is what being a co-owner is — it is already compensated through the equity mechanisms and is not separately tracked.

按各人本金占比（pro-rata）从运营基金盈余中偿还，先偿还，再有任何对任何人的分配。花在团体工作上的时间，本身就是作为共同所有者的一部分——它已经通过股权机制得到补偿了，不会被单独再计一次。

Repayment priority偿还优先级

  1. Cover ongoing operating costs as they arise
  2. 覆盖正在发生的日常运营成本
  3. Maintain the minimum reserve (three months of operating costs)
  4. 维持最低储备（三个月的运营成本）
  5. Repay capital contributions pro-rata until balances zero
  6. 按本金占比偿还资本贡献，直到所有余额清零
  7. After all capital is repaid, surplus flows into standard distribution mechanisms
  8. 所有资本偿还完之后，盈余进入标准分配机制

Distribution Schedule分配时间表

Expense-first, monthly distributions, minimum reserve maintained.

先算开支、按月分配、始终保持最低储备。

All operational expenses are paid as they arrive. Production expenses are not paid by the collective at all — those are borne by Type 1 medium managers and tracked through the recoup ledger. The monthly distribution is of net surplus only.

所有运营开支按发生即支付的方式处理。制作开支根本不由整个团体来出——那些由 Type 1 媒介负责人承担，并通过回收账本追踪。每月分配的，只是净盈余。

A minimum reserve — three months of operating costs — must be maintained at all times. Distributions are only made from surplus above this reserve. Months with no surplus above reserve simply skip distribution.

必须始终保持一个最低储备——三个月的运营成本。任何分配只从这个储备之上的盈余中出。某个月如果没有任何高于储备的盈余，那个月就直接跳过分配。

Still to Be Clarified仍待澄清的事项

This document covers the structural backbone of the model — the mechanisms that distribute revenue, recognise contribution, handle status changes. There are several important areas we have not unpacked in this pack, by design. We already have working positions on most of them. They're best clarified in conversation with the founding cohort rather than written into a long document at this stage — overloading this pack with the details would muddle more than it explains. The list below is to flag what those open conversations are, not to suggest we have no view on them.

这份文档涵盖的是这套模型的结构骨架——分配收益、认可贡献、处理状态变化的那些机制。还有几个重要的方面我们有意没有在这份资料包里展开。其中大部分我们已经有了工作中的立场。它们更适合在与创始班底的对话中去澄清，而不是在现阶段就写进一份很长的文档里——把这些细节都堆进资料包，对理解的妨碍多过帮助。下面这份清单的作用是标记出那些等着展开的对话有哪些，而不是要表达"我们对这些事还没有任何想法"。

Portfolio rights作品集使用权

Can a collaborator publicly display their work as part of their own portfolio — both during their time in the collective and after? Can they show it without naming the IP? Can they show it with the IP credited but without disclosing the collective's revenue model? What about screenshots, clips, derivative versions for showreels? The default principle should be that craft output belongs to the craftsperson for portfolio purposes, but the specifics of what counts as "the work" vs "the IP" need to be agreed.

合作者能否把自己的工作作为个人作品集的一部分公开展示——无论是在共创团体期间，还是离开之后？能否在不点名 IP 的情况下展示？能否点名 IP，但不披露团体的收益模型？截图、剪辑、为 showreel 而做的衍生版本怎么办？默认的原则应该是：作为创作产出的东西，在"作品集"层面归创作者本人所有；但"什么算作'你那部分作品'，什么算作'IP 本身'"，这条线的具体划法需要一起协商定下来。

Modification and reuse of work after leaving离开之后对原有作品的修改与再利用

When a contributor departs, can they take a track they wrote, a comic page they drew, an animation they made — and modify it, repurpose it, license it elsewhere? Can they release a non-IP version of a song with new lyrics? Can they include a stripped-down version in a film score later? The vested-share economics already protect the departing contributor's income from work they shipped; we still need to write down what they can do with the artefacts themselves.

当一位贡献者离开时，他们能否把自己写过的一首曲子、画过的一页漫画、做过的一段动画——拿去修改、再利用、授权给别处？能否在歌词全部更换之后，作为"非 IP 版本"重新发布一首歌？能否在之后的一部电影配乐里使用其简化版？已经归属（vested）的份额机制已经保护了离开者从已发布作品中获得的收入；我们还需要写下来的是：他们能对那些作品本身做什么。

Responsibilities and accountability责任与问责

Each medium manager is sovereign over their medium (Layer 3 in the creative architecture, see Transmedia Collective). But sovereignty is not unconditional — they have committed to deliver. What happens when a medium manager genuinely is not delivering, repeatedly? Who raises it? What does that conversation look like? What are the consequences? The model assumes good faith and self-regulation; it has not yet defined what happens when those break down.

每位媒介负责人对自己的媒介拥有主权（创作架构中的第 3 层，见跨媒介共创团体）。但主权不是无条件的——他们承诺要交付。如果一位媒介负责人确实在持续地无法交付，会发生什么？由谁来把这件事提出来？那场对话长什么样？后果是什么？这套模型预设的是诚意与自我调节；它还没有定义当那两样东西崩塌的时候该怎么办。

Timeline and delivery commitments时间表与交付承诺

Different mediums ship at different cadences, by design. But within each medium there are still commitments and dependencies. A composer who promised a vocal demo for the cross-medium ideation session by month-end and doesn't deliver creates real downstream cost for everyone whose work hinged on it. What's the protocol for missed timelines? Soft accountability conversations? A vote to demote tier (Low) for that period? We have a sense of how we'd handle each of these — but the actual policy is something to land on with the founding cohort.

不同媒介按不同节奏发布，这是有意的设计。但在每一种媒介内部，仍然存在承诺与相互依赖。一位作曲人答应在月底前为跨媒介构思会议交一段人声 demo，结果没交，对所有依赖这件事往下推进工作的人来说，都会造成真实的下游成本。错过时间表时的处理流程是什么？是温和的问责对话？是发起表决，将该期层级降至"低活动"？这些我们都已经有一个大致的处理思路——但具体的政策，是要和创始班底一起落地下来的。

Anything else this list missed这份清单遗漏的其他任何事项

This list is not complete. The model is genuinely young, and there will be questions we haven't anticipated yet. Whatever those are, the same posture applies: we clarify them with the people who join, before they become problems.

这份清单不是完整的。这套模型确实还很年轻，会出现一些我们目前还想不到的问题。无论是什么问题，我们都会用同样的姿态去对待：在它们变成"麻烦"之前，与加入进来的人一起把它澄清清楚。

Priority Actions Before Anyone Starts任何人开始之前的优先行动

  1. Write a term sheet写一份条款表（term sheet） covering medium taxonomy, the two-phase split, the IP equity routing and orphan pool, the earned contribution conversation process, the rolling 12-month unit mechanism, the status system, operating fund scope, capital contribution treatment, grant approach, distribution schedule, minimum reserve, departure clauses, IP contribution clause, and the IP-outlives-individuals principle. Not a full legal contract initially — clear enough that every party understands what they are agreeing to. ，覆盖：媒介分类、两阶段分配、IP 资产分流与无主池、贡献份额对话流程、12 个月滚动单位机制、状态系统、运营基金范围、资本贡献处理方式、资助处理方式、分配时间表、最低储备、离开条款、IP 贡献条款、以及"IP 比任何个人活得更久"的原则。一开始不需要是完整的法律合同——只要清楚到让每一方都明白自己在同意的是什么就够了。
  2. Get sign-off before work starts.在任何工作开始之前，先把字签了。 Nothing starts until something is signed. Creative work that starts before agreement is in place creates exactly the kind of ambiguity this structure is designed to prevent. 在签署任何东西之前，什么都不开始。在协议尚未到位时就开始的创作工作，制造出来的恰恰是这个结构想要防止的那种模糊性。
  3. Set up basic administration.把基础行政搭起来。 Operating fund needs a home, the unit accrual ledger needs to exist, the recoup ledger needs to exist from day one of any production, the operating fund capital ledger needs to exist for capital contributions. A spreadsheet and a shared folder are sufficient at early stage — but they must exist and must be maintained consistently. 运营基金需要有一个落地的地方；单位累积账本需要存在；回收账本必须从任何制作的第一天起就存在；用于记录资本贡献的"运营基金资本账本"也必须存在。早期阶段，一个电子表格和一个共享文件夹就够了——但它们必须真的存在，必须被持续地维护。
