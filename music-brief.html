Game Music Composer Brief Ritual & Ruin

Looking for a music composer and/or SFX designer to own the sound of Ritual & Ruin.

The game

A 2v2 local co-op party game for PC (Steam). Rounds run 5–10 minutes, couch multiplayer for now (online may come later, if it turns out to be feasible). You control a small creature in a 3D arena of stacked floors: hunt blood, carry it to a ritual altar, and pour it in; feeding the altar evolves your creature from a cute little thing into something far stranger. It plays as chaotic, cute, and quick to pick up, but it doesn't stay that way. Underneath the party game is something stranger and darker, with a lot of interesting room for audio to live in that turn, if you want to take it there.

The feel

Dark kawaii curdling into cosmic horror. Cute creatures, blood, and ritual altars, all observed by a cold automated lab that stopped caring a long time ago. The tone lives in tension: cute versus wrong, relief versus triumph, cooperation versus exploitation, organic versus clinical. The cute never leaves; it gets consumed. Visually it's heading toward a "clinical ritual" look: sterile lab surfaces housing something raw and ceremonial. Underneath the chaos, the theme is persistence in the face of uncertainty: how far you keep going when you don't know if it'll pay off.

The opportunity

Total authorship of the audio. This isn't a hand-off scoring gig where you get a finished game and fill it with music. You'd shape the entire soundscape (music and SFX as one sonic world) and influence the story from a sonic perspective: how the emotional arc lands, where the game holds back versus releases, how the world's slow decay actually sounds. You'd be in control of everything audio, and the game's systems can bend toward what you want rather than the other way around. For an audio person, that level of control is rare; most gigs hand you a locked thing.

Music only, or SFX only, is completely fine too; just say which.

The honest deal

  • It's a learning project. My first game, made partly to learn. The bar is just to ship something, not to make a hit.
  • Compensation is open to figuring out together, just not upfront payment. It's not that kind of gig. The default is revenue-share, and I honestly don't expect the game itself to earn much, so plan as if that's roughly zero. But there's room to get creative. For example, if we put out an OST (soundtrack), those sales can go to you. Some people may be happy to do this purely for the experience and the credit; if you need compensation, we'll find something fair. Just don't expect a cheque up front.
  • I'm aiming to ship the game by end of 2026, but there's no deadline pressure on you. Part-time, your pace, scope as big or small as you like, stop whenever; we ship with whatever audio exists at that point. Even one track that belongs in this world would make me happy.
