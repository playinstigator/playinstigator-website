Transmedia Collective

IP that lives across mediums as co-equal facets

Most transmedia fails because the "main" medium owns the IP and everything else is licensed downstream. I'm building toward a model where the song, the comic, the game, and the merch are co-equal facets of the same world — designed together from the start, not adapted after.

Role Designer · World-builder
Stage Concept · Early

Thinking

Writing that grounds the work.

Two longer essays that explain why I'm building what I'm building, and how I think about IP, medium, and the difference between entertainment that grows you and entertainment that hollows you out.

Essay · Transmedia design

IP is Experience

Most transmedia projects fail because they treat the IP as a property to be exploited across mediums, rather than an experience that exists as the sum of its facets. A thesis on medium-agnostic IP, with case studies from Kpop Demon Hunters, Star Wars, Frozen, Among Us, and The Matrix.

Entertainment for Transformation

The argument behind Play Instigator: entertainment doesn't have to be brainrotting, brainwashing, or escapist. It can explore life's difficulty with you — and that's not a niche, it's an unmet need. What it's for, why it exists, and what kind of partners I'm looking for.
