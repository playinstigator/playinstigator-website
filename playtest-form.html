Thanks for playing. What you just experienced is the very first playable version of this game — geometric placeholders, no art, no polish. I'm not testing if it's fun yet. I'm testing if the core idea works at all.

01 The Core Mechanic

At any point during the match, did you feel like you understood what you were supposed to be doing?

The main action is toggling between two modes: one to move freely, one to carry blood. Did that feel like it made sense as a mechanic?

Did anything happen during the match where you thought "oh, this is actually something"?

02 What Worked / What Didn't

What left you unsure what to do, or why something happened?

Anything — a mechanic landing, a chaotic moment, a close call — that felt like it had potential?

What's the one most important thing missing or wrong at this stage?

03 The Bigger Picture

The round was designed to last 5–10 minutes. How did it feel?

Pretend you're texting a friend about it right now. What would you say?

Setting aside the missing art and polish — does the underlying game concept seem like something worth finishing?
